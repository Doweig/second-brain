## Context

Recorded after a meeting with [[anton|Anton]] on March 11. Doweig is processing the equity/shares conversation and preparing for a meeting with Dan later that day.

## Key Points

### Shares Discussion

- [[anton|Anton]] and [[dan-aspire|Dan]] had a meeting after meeting Doweig to decide the share split.
- The push for a shares discussion came from Dan ("usually is not coming forward, but this time apparently it's coming from Dan").
- Doweig interprets this as Dan wanting to feel in control.

### Anton's Team Composition

- **Dan:** Has a gym. First tech company. ~50 years old. Wife is the CMO.
- **A young doctor:** To whom they want to give shares.
- **Anton:** Has been working on this nearly a year, going "non-stop" to get it off the ground.
- Anton also has shares allocated for his own doctors.

### Doweig's Concerns

1. **Control:** Feels uncomfortable not being in control. Self-describes as a "control freak."
2. **Dan's fit:** Questions Dan's ability to contribute meaningfully — first tech company, limited computer skills, can't build the product. Acknowledges the gym could help kickstart with early customers but doubts it's a scalable acquisition channel.
3. **CEO role:** Believes a CEO is needed. Doesn't want Dan as CEO by default just because he can't do the technical work. Feels the CEO should be whoever can raise money.
4. **Bad deal:** Feels equal shares with Dan would be a poor deal given relative contributions.
5. **Customer acquisition:** Prefers events (park meetups, community activities) over gym-based acquisition.

### Doweig on Fundraising & CEO Role

- Wants to be CEO and own strategy + [[fundraise|fundraising]].
- Previously considered getting help with fundraising but now feels ready to do it himself.
- Sees this as an important founder milestone: "I feel like it's an important step in a founder's life to actually try to raise."
- Prior experience: was part of one company that tried to raise and one that almost successfully raised.

### Doweig's CEO Philosophy

- "Whoever can raise the most money should be the CEO."
- Building skill, empathy, public speaking are secondary to bringing in capital.

### Next Steps (at time of recording)

- Meeting Dan later that day (March 11) to discuss further.
- Wants to understand how much Dan actually knows about the project.
