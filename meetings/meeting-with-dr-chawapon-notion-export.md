Summary

### Overview

Research interview exploring health checkup practices, longevity medicine, and ideal checkup design with an obesity specialist doctor from [[bnh-hospital|B&H hospital]]. Discussion covered personal health routines, patient behaviors, testing approaches, and optimal checkup frequency. 

### Doctor's Personal Health Practice

- Performs comprehensive annual checkup including blood tests for sugar, cholesterol, kidney function, liver function, hormones, and micronutrients
- Tests testosterone and growth hormone once or twice yearly; testosterone levels are borderline low but acceptable
- Uses AXA insurance with 30,000 baht annual checkup coverage; pays 100,000 baht annually for premium insurance
- Performs checkup at Thammasat Hospital Bangna (less expensive than B&H) for approximately 29,000 baht
- Self-assessment: rates personal health at 70/100, feels current routine is sustainable without excessive sacrifice

### Physical Examinations

- Includes X-ray, echocardiography, calcium score CT scan, and ultrasound annually or every 1-2 years
- Chest X-ray frequency varies: sometimes annual, sometimes every 2-3 years depending on schedule
- Ultrasound examines organs (kidney, liver, intestines) for anatomical abnormalities, lesions, stones, and foreign bodies
- DEXA scan performed once long ago; considers it optional for personal use
- Physical exams acceptable every 1-2 years, or even every 5 years for some tests like calcium score

### Time and Experience

- Full checkup requires half day commitment
- Patient recall only 20-30% of consultation information after leaving
- B&H provides summary reports 2-3 weeks post-consultation, available in app
- Future transcript-based notes could reduce paperwork and improve efficiency

### Patient Behavior Patterns

- Most B&H patients choose basic checkup packages around 10,000 baht
- Foreigners generally ask more questions than Thai patients regardless of age
- Thai patients tend to accept doctor's assessment without detailed questioning
- Middle-aged patients (30-40) most active in health management
- Increasing use of ChatGPT by patients to understand results before appointments; questions typically about supplements, diet, exercise
- Two patient groups: majority want simple "healthy/not healthy" answer; minority (especially foreigners and health-conscious individuals) seek detailed understanding

### Blood Testing Philosophy

- Standard tests: sugar, cholesterol, kidney function, liver function considered basic foundation
- Comprehensive testing includes hormones (testosterone, IGF-1, DHEA, cortisol) and micronutrients (vitamins A, D, E, zinc, folate)
- Doctor distinguishes between conventional medicine (disease detection) and functional/wellness medicine (optimization)
- Same markers interpreted differently: conventional uses disease cutpoints, wellness uses optimal ranges
- Personalized approach: focuses testing based on patient concerns and symptoms rather than blanket comprehensive panels

### Hormone and Micronutrient Testing

- Sex hormones not routinely checked in basic checkups; female hormones challenging due to menstrual cycle fluctuations
- IGF-1 considered optional; mainly useful when combined with comprehensive hormone panel
- Vitamin D commonly tested; doctor's recent test showed low levels, now supplementing
- Micronutrients (zinc, vitamin A) never tested personally but would add if designing ideal checkup
- Thyroid and parathyroid hormones often not included in standard packages

### Supplements

Daily foundation supplements: vitamin D, probiotics, omega-3 fish oil, vitamin C  

- Takes multivitamin occasionally, not daily
- Probiotics important for gut health, immunity, reducing inflammation, and improving nutrient absorption
- Uses various probiotic brands interchangeably
- Tried continuous glucose monitor twice using Chinese brand; Aidex (A-I-D-E-X) now available in Thailand

### Gut Microbiome Testing

- Performed stool test once (appears to use GI-MAP), results showed some imbalance but good function
- Cautions that interpretation varies between labs; different technologies produce different reports
- Believes gut microbiome tests should combine with functional gut health markers, not just bacterial patterns
- Shotgun sequencing most popular current technology for comprehensive bacterial screening
- Results challenged by lack of large Asian datasets accounting for diet, age, ethnicity, and geography differences

### Ideal Checkup Design for Doctor

**Annual comprehensive checkup:** 

- Complete blood panel including additional hormones (IGF-1, DHEA, cortisol, testosterone)
- Full micronutrient testing (zinc, vitamins A, D, E, folate)
- Physical examinations (X-ray, echo, ultrasound, calcium score CT)
- Not concerned about annual X-ray radiation exposure; comparable to or less than airplane flight

**Every 3-6 months basic monitoring:**  

- Cholesterol, sugar, kidney function, liver function
- CRP (inflammation marker) for ongoing monitoring
- Vitamin D while levels normalizing
- Could be done via home nurse visit for convenience

**Physical exams:** Every 1-2 years acceptable, some tests like calcium score every 5 years sufficient 

### Checkup Package Strategy for Patients

Would offer tiered packages: 

- Small/basic packets for routine screening
- Standard comprehensive packets for middle-aged health-conscious patients
- Premium comprehensive packets including all testing aspects
- Personalization based on patient health concerns, medical history, family history, lifestyle, and specific symptoms

### Pricing Strategy and Market Analysis

- Discussed developing a comprehensive blood test service to compete with hospital checkup packages in Thailand
- Compared pricing across different markets: [[mito-health|Mito Health]] (US) offers 100+ biomarkers for $500 (~15,000 baht), while Thailand costs would be 50,000+ baht for similar tests

- Goal is to offer premium-level testing at around 9,500 baht, significantly cheaper than BDMS premium packages (~40,000 baht)
- Plan to operate tests at cost initially to acquire customers, then monetize through supplements and follow-up services (Silicon Valley strategy)

### Test Selection and Package Design

- Discussed which biomarkers to include, with debate about comprehensive vs. essential testing
- Key markers discussed: IGF-1, HSCRP (inflammation), DHEA, cortisol, vitamin D (total vs D2/D3 separately)
- Vitamin D2/D3 separate testing is expensive (2,500 baht) and may not be necessary - total vitamin D is usually sufficient
- Some expensive tests like micronutrient comprehensive panels (12,000 baht) may be optional rather than included in base package
- Medical consultant recommends starting with system-based approach (like Superpower) rather than copying existing packages, focusing on tests that can be interpreted meaningfully

### Thailand Healthcare Market Specifics

- Thailand has 70 million people vs. much larger US market, affecting test pricing
- Lab test prices in Thailand are similar to other ASEAN countries but higher than expected
- Full checkup centers require expensive equipment (MRI ~20-50M baht, CT scan ~10-20M baht, DEXA ~2M baht)
- [[nhealth|N Health]] provides free pickup service for blood samples across Thailand
- At-home nurse visits cost 800-1,500 baht

### Partnership and Logistics Discussion

- Planning to partner with N Health lab for testing - they provide sample tubes for free
- N Health offers pickup service at no charge, typically within 1-4 hours
- Medical consultant already has established relationships with nurses and N Health for home blood draw services
- Discussed potential partnerships with checkup centers like Ad Life, though they may be competitive rather than collaborative

### Alternative Revenue Streams Explored

- Discussed DEXA scan potential as standalone service - equipment costs only ~2M baht
- DEXA scans are recommended once yearly for longevity tracking, take ~15 minutes

- Medical consultant suggests DEXA center combined with longevity program has growth potential

### Product Development Approach

- Discussed two-round testing approach: basic panel first, then targeted follow-up testing based on initial results - could reduce costs
- However, nurse visit costs (800-1,500 baht) make single comprehensive visit more economical
- AI will be used for interpreting results and generating supplement/dietary plans, not for test selection
- Emphasis on quality interpretation and actionable information being key differentiator

- Tool should allow categorization by body system and easy test selection with automatic price calculation
- Medical consultant to manually review and suggest tests from [[function-health|Function Health]]'s model as starting point
