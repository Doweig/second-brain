# Meeting with Anton — 2026-03-04

Met [[anton|Anton]] through a biohacker group introduction. Planned as lunch, ran 2.5 hours. Anton is building a nearly identical blood testing + AI product in Thailand. Discussed potential merger.

- **Has raised before** (previous ventures)

## Anton's Team

- **Anton:** Tech/product (CTO type)
- **[[dan-aspire|Dan]]:** Semi-famous personal trainer in Bangkok, has his own gym. Thai wife who is reportedly very good at marketing — helped build the gym
- **Doctor:** Young (late 20s), currently finishing PhD. Not much work experience but reportedly strong with AI. Lacks credentials/seniority
- **Structure:** Anton + Dan as core founders, doctor as junior/minority. Third co-founder position open

## Their Product / Stage

- Blood testing company, similar model to Function Health / Superpower
- Planning to use Dan's clinic as umbrella (clinic not yet open — targeting June)
- **Blood panel concerns:** Planning 3,000 baht for ~30-40 markers — Guillaume identified this as essentially a basic checkup, not comprehensive longevity testing. Likely haven't worked deeply with the doctor on panel design yet
- Anton also doing premium 1:1 coaching (daily sessions + food + blood test)
- Very early stage, no product yet

## Market Intel from Anton

- **Superpower** allegedly planning to enter Thailand next year (insider knowledge — Anton knows someone at Superpower)
- Australian company also looking to start in Thailand (via [[swit|Swit]])
- Another founder in Thailand also building similar company (via Anton)
- **Conclusion:** Thai market will saturate quickly. Multiple entrants converging

## Merger Discussion

- Both very early stage, both unfunded
- Anton believes in 3-founder team; his 3rd slot is open
- Proposed: Guillaume + Anton + Dan as co-founders, doctor(s) as minority shareholders
- **Guillaume's concerns:**
  - Losing control
  - Team would be all foreigners (no deep Thai market penetration)
  - Dan's wife is Thai (can hold shares), Guillaume's wife is Thai too — dual structure possible
- **Potential argument for merger:** The real competition is well-funded international teams, not each other. Merging = stronger position to launch first and raise

## Guillaume's Takeaways

- **Urgency amplified** — market is not empty, multiple competitors converging on Thailand
- Need to move fast: blow some cash on marketing, hire a PA/marketing assistant
- Continue moving forward with [[art-chawapon-kidhirunkul|Dr. C]] regardless
- Southeast Asia domination unrealistic near-term — focus on taking Thailand
- Foreign teams' advantage = funding. Local teams' advantage = already started, lab relationships, ground presence
- If merger happens, need to frame it as "united against international competition" not "fighting each other for scraps"

## Weaknesses Identified in Anton's Approach

- Blood panel is basic (~30-40 markers for 3K baht) — essentially a checkup, not longevity
- Doctor lacks experience and credentials (late 20s, still in PhD)
- Clinic not operational yet (June ETA)
- Team is all expats except junior doctor — potential weakness for Thai market depth
- May not have deeply researched functional health / longevity-specific biomarkers
