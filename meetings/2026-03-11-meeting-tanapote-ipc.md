## Key Decisions & Guidance

### Corporate Structure — Start Thai, Convert Later

Tanapote recommends starting as a **Thai company** (not foreign) to avoid legal barriers and begin operations quickly. The hybrid approach:

1. **Now:** Incorporate as Thai company — Guillaume 49%, Thai shareholders (Yoai + Dr. C) 51%
2. **Simultaneously:** Apply for BOI Category 8.1.1
3. **When BOI approved:** Foreign co-founders inject new capital, acquire shares (possibly buying Yoai's share), company converts to foreign status — acceptable under BOI scheme

This resolves the timing problem: operate immediately while BOI processes (up to 6 months).

### Nominee Risk — Wife Structure Acceptable

Tanapote's position: **Guillaume + Yoai as co-shareholders is NOT a nominee arrangement** because:
- Both are shareholders (not Yoai alone)
- They are legally married
- Not the pattern authorities target (which is: foreign national marries Thai person from different background solely to use their name)

**Critical requirement:** Each shareholder must invest proportional capital from their own funds.
- If 2M THB total: Guillaume max 49% = 980K THB from his account
- Yoai's share must come from a separate source — **not from Guillaume's account**
- Solution: Yoai borrows from her sister and invests that money

If all money came from Guillaume → Yoai would truly be a nominee. The separate source of funds is what makes the distinction.

### Capital Structure

- Only **25% of declared capital** must be paid initially
- Remainder callable over time (e.g., another 25% at 6 months, final 50% at year end)
- If company generates profit early, can use profits for salaries instead of calling more capital
- With 2M THB declared: initial injection = 500K THB (split proportionally)

### Work Permit

- **Standard:** 4 Thai employees : 1 foreigner, 2M THB capital
- **Marriage guideline (not law):** 2 Thai employees, 1M THB capital — "guideline, not guaranteed"
- Must pay at least 25% of declared capital into company account
- Guillaume needs work permit if presenting as working for the company (not just "showing profile at events")
- Driver can count as Thai employee

### Contract Structure — Single Contract with Clinic

Tanapote confirmed: **one contract with Dr. C's clinic** covers everything, since the clinic already has contracts with nurses and lab.

**Service flow:**
1. Customer signs up via [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] app
2. BodyCypher sends instructions to clinic
3. Clinic dispatches nurse for blood draw
4. Lab processes samples
5. Results return to BodyCypher
6. AI analysis performed
7. Doctor validates results
8. Final results delivered to customer

**Payment flow:** Customer pays BodyCypher directly (online payment). Clinic is a contractor.

**Liability:** Clinic/doctor responsible for ensuring all outsourced services (nurses, lab, equipment) comply with Thai law.

### AI / SaMD Classification — Preliminary View

Tanapote's preliminary assessment: if the AI provides **preliminary analysis requiring doctor confirmation** (not final diagnosis), it should qualify as a **tool, not a medical device**. He will formally consult Thai FDA to confirm.

His framing to the FDA: "This is preliminary analysis that needs confirmation and verification by doctors" → should be acceptable.

### Phased Approach Agreed

Guillaume proposed and Tanapote agreed: validate key legal points before incorporation, but don't wait for everything. Timeline:
- 1-2 more calls to confirm critical points
- Incorporate in April (before or after Songkran)
- Phased product development — start with forms, consent, payment, blood draw, lab results, PDPA compliance
- AI analysis comes later
