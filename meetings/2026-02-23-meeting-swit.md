### Guillaume's Health Tech Vision

Guillaume is building a health tech startup focused on longevity and preventive care, with two core pillars: longevity science and AI. Started the project in January 2025 and currently operating solo while searching for co-founders.

**Core Product Concept:**

- Blood testing with 100+ biomarkers (5x more than traditional checkups which only test ~20)
- AI-powered personalized health recommendations, similar to Function Health and [[superpower|Superpower]] models
- Focus on preventive medicine rather than disease detection
- Custom supplement recommendations based on actual blood test results

**Vision:** Make personalized preventive care accessible to everyone through AI, providing the equivalent of having a full-time doctor and team of scientists (like Bryan Johnson) at a fraction of the cost. The goal is to enable people to optimize health, delay aging, and potentially stop aging in the future.

**Current Stage:** Research phase transitioning to customer discovery and validation. No customers served yet. Working with doctors to develop proper blood test panels. Can self-finance product-market fit phase (estimated 1-3 million baht), planning to raise seed funding for scaling.

### Product Strategy Discussion

Guillaume currently does personal blood testing every 6 months, adjusting frequency when changing routines or trying new supplements. Emphasized that after testing regularly, it seems "crazy" that people take supplements without checking their blood first.

**Key Biomarker Example:** Traditional checkups only test fasting glucose to detect diabetes, but comprehensive testing checks insulin levels to detect pre-diabetic conditions 5-10 years earlier, allowing lifestyle interventions before medication becomes necessary.

**Monetization Strategy:** Following industry pattern where companies either start with supplements and add testing, or start with testing and add supplements. Custom supplements based on actual test results would be primary revenue stream.

**Competitive Landscape:** US-based competitors (Function Health, Superpower) unlikely to expand to Southeast Asia for 5+ years, giving time to build brand loyalty and customer data moat.
