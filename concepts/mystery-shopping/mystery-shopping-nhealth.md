# Mystery Shopping Evaluation – nHealth (Rama 4)

*Date of visit: January 22, 2026*
*Package purchased: 35-biomarker panel – 3,400 THB (via Gowabi)*
*Clinic tier: Budget (lab-direct, no consultation)*

---

## 1. Pre-Visit Experience

**How did you book?** [[gowabi|Gowabi]] (third-party booking platform). Could also book directly via nHealth's LINE account.

**Booking friction:** 4/5 (painful)
- Booking itself was easy once a package was selected, but package selection was a nightmare. [[nhealth|nHealth]] lists 20–30+ packages with no clear guidance on which to choose.
- Pricing was transparent on Gowabi (per-package).
- No upselling during booking — it's self-service.
- No advance booking required. Booked and was told to show up any day. Could theoretically walk in fasted, pick a package at the counter, and go.

**Pre-visit communication:**
- Fasting requirement was specified in the package listing on Gowabi.
- Gowabi sent a confirmation email.
- No direct communication from nHealth itself.

**Key pain point:** Had to print the package listing page as a PDF and use an LLM to extract and compare all packages just to decide which one to buy. Massive friction for anyone without that technical ability.

---

## 2. Arrival & Check-in

**Time:** Arrived at 9:00 AM, started blood draw shortly after registration.

**First impression:** The Rama 4 location is on a main road with a dedicated parking lot right in front of the entrance — convenient by car. The facility feels more premium and quieter than city-center labs because it's slightly suburban, set back from the street behind the parking area. Clean, modern interior.

**Check-in process:**
- Registration was fast. Gave booking number, received a paper form to confirm name, allergies, basic info.
- All paper-based — no digital forms.
- English proficiency of staff: 3/5 — staff didn't speak great English verbally, but all paperwork was in English, so no real issues.
- They confirmed which tests were included in the package.

## 3. Testing Process

**Total stations/stops:** 1 (blood draw only)

**Flow description:** Package selection confirmed at reception → moved directly to blood draw area. Very streamlined, no additional stations.

| Blood draw | Minimal | ~10 min | 4 | Professional, efficient |

**Blood draw experience:**

- Very little verbal communication — mostly transactional.

**Assembly line feeling:** 3/5 — efficient and impersonal, but not unpleasant. It's a lab, not a wellness clinic, so expectations were calibrated accordingly.

## 5. Results Delivery

**When did you receive results?** Same day or next day, via email.

**Format:** PDF report via email.

**Quality of the report:**
- Number of biomarkers included: 35
- Were reference ranges included? Yes
- Were results flagged (high/low)? Yes — flagged in bold
- Was there any written interpretation or just raw numbers? Raw numbers with reference ranges only. No written interpretation.
- Was there an action plan or recommendations section? No

**Report quality notes:** Compared to other Thai clinics, the PDF is relatively clear. Almost entirely in English (minimal Thai). Clean layout, easy to read.

**What was MISSING from the report:**
- Any interpretation of results
- Actionable recommendations or next steps
- Context for what flagged values mean
- Suggested follow-up timeline

## 8. The BodyCypher Lens

**What would [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] do better here?**
- Home collection via mobile nurse — eliminate the need to visit the lab entirely
- Provide meaningful interpretation of results with actionable recommendations
- Simplify package selection — the 20–30 package maze is a huge friction point that BodyCypher can solve with curated, opinionated panels

**What should BodyCypher steal/copy from this experience?**
- The parking/facility setup: being set back from the street behind a parking lot creates a more premium, quieter feeling vs. street-level city-center labs
- Speed and efficiency of the process — in and out quickly, no unnecessary padding
- Clean English-language PDF reports with clear flagging of abnormal values

**If this person were handed a BodyCypher action plan after this visit, what would they value most?**
- Interpretation of all 35 markers with plain-language explanations and personalized action items — the one thing completely missing from the nHealth experience

**Key quote or moment that captures the experience:**
> "Under 100 baht per biomarker, in and out, zero hand-holding. If you know what you're looking at, it's unbeatable. If you don't, you're on your own."
