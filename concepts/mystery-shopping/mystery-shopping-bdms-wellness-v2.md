## 4. Doctor Consultation

There were TWO consultations: one before testing (package selection) and one after (results review, ~2 weeks later).

### Consultation 1: Pre-Test (Package Selection)

**Consultation duration:** Extended (felt like a long, natural conversation)

**Doctor's approach:**
- Showed all available packages and tests (blood panels, DNA testing, more)
- Chatted extensively, asked questions
- Also discussed Doweig's [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] project
- Did not feel rushed at all
- Lots of options to choose from, potentially overwhelming

### Consultation 2: Results Review (~2 weeks later)

**Wait time before seeing doctor:** [Not captured]

**Consultation duration:** ~1.5 hours

**Doctor's approach:**
- Reviewed results in front of Doweig, broadcasting the PDF from his iPad to a TV screen
- Used a stylus to annotate the PDF in real time (circling values, writing brief notes)
- Went through every single marker and explained what they meant
- Asked questions, allowed questions back
- Did not feel rushed - the opposite, it was very thorough

**But the core problem emerged:** Despite 1.5 hours of detailed verbal explanation, the takeaway was minimal. "I just don't remember everything. It's just too much information. It needs to be written." The annotated PDF amounted to circles with one or two words: "drink more water" or similar. Not a real action plan. After 1-2 weeks, almost everything discussed was forgotten.

## 5. Results Delivery

**When did you receive results?** ~1 week for lab results to be ready. However, the PDF was not proactively sent. Doweig had to ask multiple times before receiving it, and ultimately couldn't get results before the follow-up appointment (~2 weeks later). The clinic seemed to forget or deprioritize sending results between appointments.

**Format:** PDF (annotated by Dr. W on iPad during consultation, then shared). No portal or app-based delivery.

**Quality of the report:**
- Number of biomarkers included: [Not captured exactly, but described as "pretty thorough" - likely 40-50+ given the 39,000 THB price point and BDMS Wellness packaging]
- Were reference ranges included? Yes (standard lab PDF format)
- Were results flagged (high/low)? Yes (standard lab flagging)
- Was there any written interpretation or just raw numbers? Just raw numbers + Dr. W's minimal stylus annotations (circles, 1-2 word notes). No structured written interpretation.
- Was there an action plan or recommendations section? **No.** This is the critical gap. Despite a 1.5-hour consultation, the written output was a standard lab PDF with scribbled annotations. No executive summary, no dietary guidance, no supplement recommendations, no prioritized action items.

**What was MISSING from the report:**
- Any structured written action plan
- Prioritized recommendations (what to tackle first)
- Specific supplement recommendations with dosing
- Dietary guidance tied to specific markers
- Explanation of what markers mean in plain language
- Optimal ranges vs. just standard lab reference ranges
- Anything the user could reference weekly to guide behavior

## 8. The BodyCypher Lens

**What would BodyCypher do better here?**
- Deliver a structured, written action plan that captures everything Dr. W explained verbally but in a format you can reference weekly. This is the #1 gap. The 1.5-hour consultation was genuinely valuable in the moment but worthless two weeks later.
- Proactively send results as soon as they're ready, not require the patient to chase the PDF multiple times.
- Provide supplement recommendations even when markers are "not too far out of range" but still sub-optimal. Dr. W's conservative threshold left value on the table. BodyCypher's optimization-focused ranges would catch what standard clinical judgment misses.
- Track results longitudinally so the next test isn't an isolated event but part of a trend line.

**What should BodyCypher steal/copy from this experience?**
- The extended, conversational doctor consultation style. Dr. W spent real time, didn't rush, and created genuine rapport. This is what "human in the loop" should feel like. BodyCypher's doctor review should aim for this quality of engagement, even if the format shifts from 1.5 hours in person to a shorter, more focused written + call hybrid.
- The iPad-to-TV PDF annotation approach is clever for in-person delivery. The concept (visual walkthrough of results) could translate to an in-app guided tour of the action plan.
- The supplement compounding lab visibility. Showing the "how" of personalized supplements builds trust. BodyCypher could do a version of this digitally: show users how their specific results map to their specific supplement protocol.
- BDMS Wellness's facility quality sets the trust bar. BodyCypher doesn't need marble lobbies, but the app and action plan need to feel equally premium.

**If this person were handed a BodyCypher action plan after this visit, what would they value most?**
- Having everything Dr. W said written down in a structured, searchable, referable format. The entire experience validated BodyCypher's core thesis: the doctor was great, the testing was thorough, but the output format (oral advice + scribbled PDF) destroys the value within weeks.

**Key quote or moment that captures the experience:**
> "Talking is good. You feel good when you get out. But really after a week or two, the feedback gets worse because all you have is a very complex PDF and you just don't remember everything. I just don't remember anything that we talked about."

## Addendum: Supplement Upsell Observations

The supplement dynamic at BDMS Wellness is worth noting separately because it directly validates BodyCypher's model:

- BDMS Wellness has an in-house supplement compounding operation (visible lab, custom formulations)
- Previous visits included supplement recommendations/upselling
- This visit, Dr. W chose NOT to recommend supplements despite the patient actively requesting them
- Dr. W's rationale: "not too much out of range" by standard clinical thresholds
- Patient's experience: currently taking many supplements independently with noticeable positive effects
- This confirms the optimization vs. clinical threshold gap identified in customer sentiment research. Clinical doctors only act on disease-level abnormalities. Optimization-focused interpretation catches the sub-clinical improvements that matter to health-conscious users.
- **Key insight: the upsell behavior is doctor-dependent, not systematically built into the clinic's process.** This is a weakness for BDMS (inconsistent revenue capture) and an opportunity for BodyCypher (systematic, evidence-based supplement recommendations for every user).
