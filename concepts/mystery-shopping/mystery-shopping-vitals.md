# Mystery Shopping Evaluation – Vitals

*Date of visit: At-home kit (self-collection)*
*Package purchased: Hair Analysis – 1,000 THB (promotional price, normally 3,000 THB for the extensive version)*
*Clinic tier: Budget / DTC Supplement Brand*

---

## 1. Pre-Visit Experience

**How did you book?** App (had to download and register) + website (e-commerce platform)

**Booking friction:** 2/5
- Had to download app, register, then book through it. Straightforward e-commerce flow.
- Pricing was transparent — 1,000 THB and 3,000 THB tiers visible. Got the extensive version (claims ~300 markers) for 1,000 THB due to a promotional/launch discount.
- No upsell during booking itself — the upsell comes after results.
- They contacted separately for detailed shipping address (likely different fulfillment flow for the kit vs. regular supplements).

**Pre-visit communication:**
- Kit arrived with a plastic bag for hair sample collection.
- No clear instructions on whether the hair bulb/root was needed — led to plucking hair from scalp (painful) instead of potentially just cutting it.
- Instructions were minimal and ambiguous.

---

## 2. Arrival & Check-in

**N/A — At-home self-collection kit**

- Kit contents: Small plastic bag for hair sample.
- Process: Collect hair → place in bag → ship back (pre-paid return, driver pickup).
- No clinic visit, no check-in, no staff interaction at this stage.

## 4. Doctor Consultation

**Wait time before seeing doctor:** N/A — No doctor consultation

**Consultation duration:** N/A

**Doctor's approach:**
- No doctor involved at any stage.
- No doctors on their team at all (confirmed by research).
- Results delivered via email, then followed up with a phone call.

**Upselling:**
- The entire model is built around upselling supplements based on results.
- Email recommended: extra fish oil (3g/day), magnesium, garcinite, extra magnesium, and an antioxidant for carotenoid balance.
- Recommendations felt extremely generic — "you could recommend this to any man."
- Phone call followed the email: "Hey, you see your email? What do you think? Are you gonna order?" — felt disingenuous and commercially motivated.

**Questions you asked and quality of answers:**
1. Why recommend 3g fish oil when the report shows EPA/DHA levels are normal? → No satisfactory answer. Report showed only DPA as low, yet they recommended a very high dose (3g = ~5 pills/day).
2. Is hair analysis scientifically valid? → Own doctor said it's "pseudoscience" / inconclusive. Independent research suggests it's not real science.

---

## 5. Results Delivery

**When did you receive results?** A few days after sending the sample back.

**Format:** Email with report + phone call follow-up

**Quality of the report:**
- Number of biomarkers included: Claims ~300 markers
- Reference ranges: Yes, included (balance levels with green/red indicators)
- Results flagged: Yes — red dots for "focus on improving," green for normal
- Written interpretation: Minimal — mostly supplement recommendations
- Action plan: Yes, but entirely supplement-purchase-driven

**What was MISSING from the report:**
- Any clinical validation or doctor review
- Explanation of methodology (how hair = these biomarker levels?)
- Context for recommendations (why 3g fish oil when EPA/DHA showed normal?)
- Any lifestyle, diet, or non-supplement recommendations
- Scientific references or citations
- Internal consistency — report contradicts its own recommendations (omega-3 levels normal → recommends high-dose fish oil anyway)

## 8. The BodyCypher Lens

**What would [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] do better here?**
- Use validated science (blood testing) instead of pseudoscience (hair analysis)
- Have actual doctors review and interpret results
- Provide recommendations that are consistent with the test data — not generic upsells
- Offer lifestyle and dietary advice, not just supplement sales

**What should BodyCypher steal/copy from this experience?**
- **At-home convenience** — people strongly prefer not going to a clinic. The kit-to-door model removes huge friction (no needles, no travel, no scheduling)
- **App-first onboarding** — making you download the app during booking locks in the relationship early
- **The "novelty" factor works** — hair testing generated word-of-mouth buzz. Several people asked about it. Novel testing methods drive organic marketing
- **Low-cost entry point** — 1,000 THB is very accessible. Gets people in the door even if the real revenue is downstream

**If this person were handed a BodyCypher action plan after this visit, what would they value most?**
- Scientific credibility and internal consistency — recommendations that actually match the data
- A real doctor or qualified professional explaining the results
- Honest, non-commercial supplement advice (with dosing that makes sense)

**Key quote or moment that captures the experience:**
> "They sell supplements. They don't have any doctor on the team. So anything science-related, they really have to make me trust them — and right now I think it's just bogus."
