# Mystery Shopping Evaluation — Pathlab

*Date of visit: January 22, 2026*
*Package purchased: Men Health Profile (Diamond) + add-ons — ฿7,830 total*
*Clinic tier: Budget*

---

## 1. Pre-Visit Experience

**How did you book?** Contacted online (LINE/chat). They told me to just show up — no appointment needed.

**Booking friction:** 2 (fairly seamless)
- One interaction online, then walk in. Minimal steps.
- Pricing was transparent in-person — they have a massive printed book with every individual test and pre-made packages, so you can hand-pick or choose a package. However, no easily accessible online version (asked for one, was told no). Some gatekeeping — you can't find pricing by yourself before visiting.
- They did try to upsell — not a bigger package, but a **prepaid follow-up**: pay for two of the same package at a discount, then use the second one in 3–6 months. Smart upsell strategy — assumes the customer already researched their package choice and doesn't try to change their mind, just encourages repeat testing.
- No advance booking needed — just show up.

**Pre-visit communication:**
- Online communication was clear and straightforward.
- Format: LINE/online chat.
- Instructions were adequate (fasting, etc.).

---

## 2. Arrival & Check-in

**Time:** Arrived mid-morning (~10–11 AM), started testing shortly after.

**First impression:** Small clinic, not fancy, but great location — right next to Emporium on Sukhumvit (Phrom Phong area). The location is a definite selling point. Not a premium hospital feel, but clean and functional — gets the job done.

**Check-in process:**
- Quick registration — arrive, pay, get started.
- All paper forms — consent forms, invoices, everything on paper. Felt low-tech.
- Language: Everything was in English. Staff clearly comfortable working in English. (4/5 English proficiency)
- They confirmed the tests included in the package.

---

## 3. Testing Process

**Total stations/stops:** 2 (blood draw + urine collection)

**Flow description:** Arrive → pay → blood draw → urine sample → done. Very streamlined given the small clinic size.

| Station | Wait Time | Duration | Staff Friendliness (1-5) | Notes |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| Blood draw | Minimal (one seat, wait if occupied) | ~10 min | 4 | Professional, efficient |
| Urine sample | None | ~5 min | 4 | Quick and straightforward |

**Blood draw experience:**
- Professional. No issues.
- More thorough draw than N Health for a comparable price point — good number of vials.
- Did not note exact vial count, but the panel was comprehensive for the price.

**Assembly line feeling?** 2 (fairly personal) — The small clinic size actually works in its favor here. Only one blood draw seat, one room. It doesn't feel like cattle herding — not because they designed it that way, but because the volume is naturally low.

---

## 4. Doctor Consultation

**Wait time before seeing doctor:** N/A — no doctor consultation offered as part of the visit.

**Consultation duration:** 0 minutes.

**Doctor's approach:**
- No doctor involvement whatsoever. When asked about result interpretation, staff said they could analyze results online after delivery. When asked who would do the interpretation, the answer was "me" — a nurse/technician, not a physician.
- No results review, no explanation, no recommendations, no lifestyle questions.

**Upselling:**
- The only upsell was the prepaid follow-up package (described above in pre-visit). No doctor-driven upselling of additional tests or supplements.

**Questions you asked and quality of answers:**
1. "Can you interpret my results?" → "Yes, we can do that online after you get them." Interpretation would be done by a nurse/technician, not a doctor.

**Note:** Could potentially go back with results and ask for interpretation as a follow-up test of the service.

---

## 5. Results Delivery

**When did you receive results?** Same day for most biomarkers. One exception: **IGF-1 was sent out to Bumrungrad** for processing and arrived a few days later on a separate report page.

**Format:** PDF sent digitally.

**Quality of the report:**
- Number of biomarkers included: Comprehensive for the price — full panel from the Men Health Profile package plus the two add-ons (free testosterone, IGF-1).
- Were reference ranges included? Yes.
- Were results flagged (high/low)? Yes, flagging was present.
- Was there any written interpretation or just raw numbers? Just raw numbers with reference ranges and flags. No written interpretation, no narrative, no context.
- Was there an action plan or recommendations section? No.

**What was MISSING from the report:**
- Any form of interpretation or explanation of what results mean
- Actionable recommendations
- Context for flagged values
- Trend tracking or comparison capability
- The IGF-1 result came on a completely separate page from Bumrungrad, suggesting they outsource "exotic" tests — could indicate limited in-house capabilities for advanced markers

---

## 6. Follow-Up

**Did they schedule or suggest a follow-up?** Only via the prepaid discount upsell at booking. No clinical follow-up suggested based on results.

**Did they provide any way to ask questions after the visit?** Theoretically yes — they mentioned online interpretation. In practice, this would be done by a nurse/technician.

**Did they offer ongoing care or just a one-time transaction?** One-time transaction. The prepaid package upsell is the closest thing to a relationship, but it's transactional, not care-oriented.

## 8. The BodyCypher Lens

**What would [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] do better here?**
- Interpretation. This is where Pathlab (and every budget option) is consistently weakest. The data is there — the meaning is not.
- Digital experience. Everything at Pathlab is paper-based and PDF. A proper app with results tracking, trend visualization, and structured action plans would be a massive step up.
- Doctor involvement. Having an actual physician review results and provide personalized recommendations is the gap BodyCypher fills.

**What should BodyCypher steal/copy from this experience?**
- The walk-in simplicity. "Just show up, sit down, pee in there, take your blood, thank you, off you go." Low friction physical experience is great.
- Transparent à la carte pricing. The big book of individual tests with clear prices builds trust. Customers can hand-pick or use packages.
- The prepaid follow-up upsell. Smart, non-aggressive, aligned with customer interest. "Pay for two, get a discount, come back in 6 months for the same test." Better than cramming more tests into one visit.
- The location strategy. Being next to a major BTS station / mall (Emporium/Phrom Phong) is a huge convenience factor.

**If this person were handed a BodyCypher action plan after this visit, what would they value most?**
- Understanding what their numbers actually mean and what to do about them. The data quality from Pathlab is solid — the interpretation layer is completely absent.

**Key quote or moment that captures the experience:**
> "Incredible value for the biomarkers you get. Location is unbeatable. But you walk out with a PDF full of numbers and zero idea what any of it means."
