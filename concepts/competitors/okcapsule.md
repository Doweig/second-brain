# OK Capsule: B2B Supplement Manufacturing Platform Analysis

**OK Capsule is not a direct-to-consumer supplement company—it's a B2B Manufacturing-as-a-Service platform.** This critical distinction shapes everything about their business model. The company enables doctors, telehealth companies, wellness brands, and influencers to launch personalized supplement packs under their own brands without holding inventory. Founded in 2019 and based in Reno, Nevada, OK Capsule has raised approximately **$16.5 million** in funding and positions itself as the only cGMP-certified personalization co-packer adhering to FDA regulations.

---

## Website positioning reveals clear B2B focus

OK Capsule's homepage headline—**"Launch Your Supplement Brand — No Inventory, No Risk, No Hassle"**—immediately signals their B2B model. The company explicitly targets three customer segments: doctors and clinics seeking patient supplement solutions, telehealth companies wanting integrated fulfillment via API, and startups testing supplement concepts with zero risk.

Their core value propositions emphasize operational benefits for business clients: no minimum order quantities, zero inventory risk, no deposits required, and rapid speed-to-market. The company claims to offer **60+ bioavailable, clinically-backed supplements** with options to bring custom formulations. All products are manufactured in cGMP-certified, FDA-compliant facilities with third-party ISO-17025 lab testing.

The mission statement reinforces their enablement role: *"OK Capsule exists to improve wellness for everyone by enabling any business to deliver a personalized nutrition experience directly to their customers."* Their sustainability angle—**80% less plastic** than traditional bottles with compostable packaging options—serves as a secondary differentiator.

---

## Pricing architecture remains opaque

OK Capsule does not publish specific pricing tiers publicly. Their pricing page states **"Pricing That Evolves With Your Business"** and promises "transparent, all-inclusive pricing" with a per-order fee structure, but dollar amounts require consultation via an interactive calculator.

The only publicly visible pricing is their **Shopify app at $300/month** with a 30-day free trial. This app, called "OK Capsule Supplement Packs," allows merchants to build, design, and sell supplement packs within Shopify storefronts.

## Product offering centers on white-label fulfillment

**Blood Testing:** OK Capsule does not currently offer blood testing services. However, "coming soon" features announced on their Summit landing page include at-home nutrient deficiency testing and suPAR inflammation biomarker testing through a partnership with suPAR (described as "one of the most clinically validated inflammation markers in the world"). DNA testing is mentioned as an input method their clients can use for personalization, but OK Capsule does not provide DNA tests directly.

**Supplement Model:** The company operates a **white-label manufacturing-as-a-service platform**—not a marketplace or recommendation service. They manufacture supplements in their Reno, Nevada facility and offer:
- Daily dose packets (30ct or 60ct rolls) in sustainable dispenser boxes
- Custom formulations or pre-made "Solution Packs" for conditions like sleep, GLP-1 support, and longevity
- 60+ supplements including vitamins, herbs, mushrooms, and amino acids
- Available certifications: Organic, Kosher, Halal, Vegan, Non-GMO, Glyphosate-free

**App Features:** OK Capsule has **no consumer-facing mobile app** on iOS or Android. Their technology stack includes:
- Shopify App (Pack Builder) for merchant integration
- Enterprise API built on Salesforce
- Web portal (portal.okcapsule.app) for B2B clients
- Machine vision system for packet verification (QC, not personalization)

**AI Claims:** The company mentions AI-driven recommendations and processing health metrics in blog content, but specifics are limited. The primary technology focus appears to be manufacturing quality control and compliance automation rather than sophisticated personalization algorithms.

**Clinical Involvement:** Co-founder Dr. Andrew Brandeis is a practicing Naturopathic Doctor with 20+ years of experience. Supplements are described as "doctor-formulated," but OK Capsule does not offer telehealth consultations directly—that's left to their B2B clients.

## Customer signals reveal concerning patterns

**OK Capsule has no presence on consumer review platforms** because they're a B2B company. They have no iOS App Store app, no Google Play app, no Trustpilot profile, and no BBB listing.

The only public customer feedback exists on the **Shopify App Store**, where their app holds a **1.0 out of 5 stars** rating based on limited reviews:

**Active Review (January 2026):** A customer reported trying for 4 months to get the app integrated, citing poor communication, unresponsive customer service, and unresolved payment processing glitches.

**Archived Review (May 2024):** A merchant warned others not to add the app, alleging it automatically signed them up for a $300 monthly subscription without clear disclosure and repeatedly attempted to charge their account.

The Shopify app shows only **4 total installs** with a **20% year-over-year decline** in installations. All installs are in the United States across health products and food/beverage categories.

**Glassdoor employee reviews** (7 total) are generally positive, describing a collaborative environment and good work-life balance, though acknowledging startup risks around job security.

## Conclusion

OK Capsule occupies a specific niche as infrastructure for personalized supplement brands rather than competing directly with consumer supplement companies. Their **core strengths** include FDA-compliant cGMP manufacturing, a flexible no-minimum-order model, physician leadership lending credibility, and a sustainability angle with reduced plastic packaging. The company has secured meaningful venture backing and established partnerships with healthcare practices.

**Key concerns** include extremely poor Shopify app reviews suggesting integration and support challenges, very limited social media presence despite targeting influencers as customers, and undisclosed pricing requiring sales conversations. The promised blood testing features remain "coming soon" without clear timelines. For potential B2B clients evaluating OK Capsule as a fulfillment partner, the Shopify app feedback warrants serious due diligence on implementation support and billing practices.
