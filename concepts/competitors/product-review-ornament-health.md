# Product Review: Ornament Health

*Date: February 2026 | Plan: Free (previous user, reviewed via YouTube + screenshots) | Data submitted: PDF upload*
*Website: https://ornament.health/*
*HQ: Switzerland*

---

## Onboarding

**How did I submit data?** PDF upload or camera OCR (photograph a paper report and it extracts the data).
**What personal info did they ask?** Includes a questionnaire - e.g., "What has your diet been in the past three months?" with options like standard, keto, etc.
**How long from signup to first result?** Unknown (reviewed from prior use + YouTube).
**Did it recognize my lab format?** Supports PDF parsing and camera-based OCR. No details on parsing accuracy.
**First impression:** Functional but messy. Feels like it was designed by doctors for doctors.

---

## Dashboard / global view

**What's the main screen?** A "Monitor" page showing a list of all biomarker details. There's also a view organized by organ/category.
**Global score?** No global health score. They skip straight to per-organ scoring.
**Category or organ scores?** Yes - 9 categories, each scored out of 5:
1. Hormones
2. Cardiovascular
3. Immunity
4. Respiratory
5. Nerves
6. Digestion
7. Blood
8. Liver
9. Urogenital

You can filter the biomarker list by organ to see which markers map to each category.

**Visual system?** Organ scores out of 5. Biomarker ranges shown with normal (wider) and optimal (narrower) zones on a graph.
**What stands out?** The organ-based organization is a reasonable concept. The dual range system (normal vs. optimal) is good.
**What's missing?** No global score or top-level "how am I doing" view. The overview is the weakest part of the product - hard to understand, not visually clear, and not well designed.

---

## Biomarker detail

**How many biomarkers recognized?** Unknown exact count - appears to support a wide range via PDF parsing.
**Reference ranges:** Two ranges per marker - a wider "normal" range and a narrower "optimal" range. This is a meaningful feature.
**Visualization:** Graph showing where you fall relative to both ranges. Also shows:
- Population statistics (what percentage of users are in optimal, normal, slightly above/below)
- Age-based statistics (a graph with age on x-axis and biomarker value on y-axis, showing how the marker typically changes with age and where your dot sits relative to your age cohort)
- Historical trend line for your own values over time

**Explanation quality:** Per-marker explanations exist. Quality seems adequate but not exceptional. Sources/citations unclear.
**What's good about this view?** The detail view is actually the strongest part of the product. The dual ranges, population stats, age curve visualization, and personal history are all solid. Per-biomarker level, it does a good job.
**What's bad or missing?** The quality at the individual marker level doesn't translate up to the overview. The gap between "good detail view" and "bad overview" is the core design failure.

---

## Action plan / recommendations

**Is there a synthesized action plan?** Barely. They generate a text-based "conclusion report" after analyzing results, but it's not prominent and not well done.
**How is it organized?** Auto-generated paragraphs per marker that's out of range. Example format: "Risk evaluation: High risk - Metabolic and cardiovascular disease. High levels of total cholesterol and LDL indicate..."
**Is it cross-referenced across biomarkers?** No. It reads as isolated per-marker paragraphs, not a synthesized plan.
**Do they prioritize?** No clear prioritization or "start here" signal.

**Specificity (1-5):** 1/5
*Generic risk statements, not actionable protocols.*

**Personalization (1-5):** 2/5
*References your actual values but doesn't synthesize into personalized guidance.*

**What do they recommend?** They suggest additional tests you should get ("personalized checkup" feature - a list of what to check next). But this was actually a turn-off: they tell you what to test but give you no help finding where to do it. The lab discovery problem (which labs are reputable, which are scams, where to go) is real and unsolved by this product.

---

## Tracking and history

**Longitudinal trends?** Yes - graphs and tables showing your values over time per biomarker.
**Can I upload older results?** Yes, via PDF upload or camera OCR.
**Trend visualization quality:** Decent at the individual marker level. The history view within each biomarker detail page works.

---

## AI / interactive features

**AI chat or Q&A?** Yes - they have an AI you can ask questions to. It appears to have access to your results. Quality/depth unclear from review.
**Other notable features?** Wiki section with detailed per-marker explanations. "Personalized checkup" feature suggesting what tests to order next. Camera OCR for paper reports.

---

## Supplement sales

**Do they sell supplements?** No.
**Upsell aggressiveness (1-5):** 1/5
*No supplement sales at all.*

---

## Overall

**Design quality (1-5):** 2/5
*Functional but ugly. Messy layout. Feels like a doctor's tool, not a consumer product. The detail views are okay but the top-level experience is poorly designed.*

**Mobile experience:** App (iOS/Android). Usable but not polished.

**Best thing about this product:** The per-biomarker detail view. Dual ranges (normal + optimal), population statistics, age-based curves, and personal history are all solid features. At the individual marker level, they do a good job.

**Biggest gap or disappointment:** Everything above the biomarker level. The overview/dashboard is messy and hard to understand. The action plan is basically non-existent - just auto-generated risk paragraphs per out-of-range marker. No synthesized recommendations, no supplement guidance, no operational specificity. The "personalized checkup" feature tells you what to test next but leaves you completely stranded on where to actually go do it.

**What should [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]] steal?**
- Dual reference ranges (normal + optimal) per biomarker
- Age-based curve visualization (showing how a marker typically changes with age and where you sit)
- Population statistics per marker (what % of users are in optimal/normal/out of range)
- Organ/system-based categorization of biomarkers (9 categories is a reasonable taxonomy to study)

**What should BodyCypher NOT copy?**
- The ugly, messy dashboard design
- Auto-generated per-marker risk paragraphs as a substitute for a real action plan
- Telling users what to test next without helping them find where to do it
- The "designed by doctors for doctors" aesthetic - BodyCypher needs to feel like a consumer product

**Overall rating (1-10):** 4/10
*A serious product with good bones at the biomarker detail level, but it seems to have stalled. The overview is weak, the action plan is essentially non-existent, the design is dated, and it doesn't solve the "now what?" problem. Good reference for individual marker visualization patterns, but not a competitive threat and not a model to follow for the overall experience.*
