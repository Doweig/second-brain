# Hims & Hers – Competitive Analysis
*Analysis date: January 20, 2026*

**Hims & Hers has evolved from a men's ED telehealth startup into a $7+ billion wellness empire now aggressively entering diagnostics—the same territory as [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]].** The company's compounded GLP-1 weight loss program now drives their fastest growth ($725M projected for 2025), and their December 2025 acquisition of YourBio Health signals a strategic push into at-home blood testing. With 2.4 million subscribers, $2.3+ billion in projected 2025 revenue, and celebrity partnerships from Jennifer Lopez to Rob Gronkowski, they've mastered the playbook for destigmatizing sensitive health topics through direct-to-consumer telehealth. Their biggest vulnerability: customer complaints about subscription traps and regulatory risk around compounded medications.

### Target customer profile
The primary audience is **tech-savvy millennials and Gen Z aged 25–45** who value convenience, privacy, and affordability. These are middle to upper-middle-class consumers frustrated with traditional healthcare barriers—long wait times, expensive co-pays, and the embarrassment of discussing sensitive conditions in person. Imagery and messaging emphasize a modern, approachable aesthetic that normalizes conversations around ED, hair loss, and mental health.

### Key differentiators (as they position themselves)
1. **100% online convenience** — No in-person visits required, asynchronous messaging with providers
2. **Destigmatization** — "Supporting care for conditions that patients often feel uncomfortable talking about"
3. **Affordable cash-pay pricing** — No insurance needed; GLP-1s at $199/month vs. $1,000+ for branded Wegovy
4. **Personalization** — MedMatch AI system identifies optimal treatments from millions of data points
5. **Licensed provider network** — Board-certified providers in all 50 states
6. **Discreet delivery** — Plain packaging, free 2-day shipping

### Brand evolution
Hims launched in 2017 focused narrowly on **erectile dysfunction and hair loss** for men. The expansion trajectory has been aggressive:
- **2018:** Launched Hers (women's platform with birth control, skincare)
- **2020:** Added mental health services and primary care
- **2021:** Went public; acquired Apostrophe (skincare) and Honest Health (UK)
- **2024:** Launched compounded GLP-1 weight loss program—now their fastest-growing category
- **2025:** Added lab testing, low testosterone treatment, menopause offerings; expanded to UK, Canada, Europe

**Weight loss has now overtaken sexual health as the primary growth driver**, projected to generate $725 million in 2025 revenue.

| **Skincare (Rx)** | $29/month | $50/month (bundle) | — |
| **Lab Testing** | TBD (launching 2026) | — | — |

### Entry points and pricing hooks
The lowest advertised prices are designed for conversion:
- **"$4/dose"** for ED medication captures attention
- **"$69/month"** oral weight loss kit positions against $1,000+ branded GLP-1s
- **"$199/month"** compounded semaglutide is the flagship hook—90% cheaper than Wegovy
- **Free consultations** remove friction from first engagement

### Subscription structure and upsells
All pricing favors longer commitments:
- **Monthly:** Highest per-unit cost, maximum flexibility
- **3-month:** 15–20% savings
- **6-month:** 25–30% savings
- **10–12-month:** 35–50% savings (lowest advertised prices require this commitment)

Cross-sell strategy bundles complementary products: ED + hair loss, ED + heart health (tadalafil + atorvastatin), "Hair Power Pack" combining finasteride, minoxidil, shampoo, and biotin. Over **55% of subscribers now have at least one personalized subscription**, indicating successful upselling.

### Pricing strategy observations
Hims uses **decoy pricing** (high-priced brand-name options make generics look attractive), **commitment-based discounting** (longest plans = best price), and **bundling** to increase average order value (up 63% to $168). The model optimizes for subscription lock-in over one-time purchases—**90%+ of revenue is subscription-based**.

## Product & Service Details

### Categories offered

**Men (Hims):**
- Sexual health (ED, premature ejaculation, low testosterone)
- Hair loss
- Weight loss / GLP-1s
- Mental health (anxiety, depression)
- Skincare
- Primary care (30+ conditions)
- Lab testing (rolling out)

**Women (Hers):**
- Weight loss / GLP-1s
- Hair thinning
- Mental health
- Skincare / acne
- Birth control
- Menopause / hormone therapy
- Lab testing (rolling out)

### Telehealth model
The process is entirely asynchronous for most services:
1. Customer completes online health questionnaire
2. Licensed provider (in patient's state) reviews within hours
3. Provider determines if prescription is appropriate
4. Medication ships free with discreet packaging
5. Ongoing 24/7 messaging with providers included

**Video consultations** are available for some services (mental health therapy at $99/session, primary care at $39/visit). Consultations are free—no upfront fee to get started.

### Prescription vs. OTC breakdown
The majority of revenue comes from **prescription medications** (finasteride, sildenafil, tadalafil, compounded semaglutide, SSRIs, tretinoin). OTC products include:
- Minoxidil (topical hair solution)
- Biotin gummies
- Melatonin supplements
- Climax delay sprays/wipes
- Basic skincare (serums, moisturizers)

### GLP-1 weight loss program (critical category)
This is now their fastest-growing segment:
- **Compounded semaglutide injections:** $199/month (6-month plan)—same active ingredient as Ozempic/Wegovy
- **Generic liraglutide:** $299/month
- **Oral weight loss kits:** $69/month (metformin, bupropion, topiramate, vitamin B12, naltrexone)
- **Branded Wegovy:** Briefly offered at $599/month via Novo Nordisk partnership (terminated June 2025)

Included in GLP-1 plans: medication, anti-nausea support, 24/7 provider messaging, app access with trackers/recipes, and personalized dosing. The company projects **$725 million in GLP-1 revenue for 2025**.

**Key risk:** Compounded medications are NOT FDA-approved for safety or efficacy. This has drawn regulatory scrutiny and led to the Novo Nordisk partnership termination.

### Lab testing (new, highly relevant for BodyCypher)
In December 2025, Hims acquired **YourBio Health**, a Boston-based company with TAP device technology for pain-free blood sampling using HALO microneedle technology. They also acquired **Trybe Labs** in February 2025.

Planned testing capabilities (rolling out 2026):
- Hormone levels
- Cardiac risk markers
- Cholesterol and LDL
- Lipoprotein(a)
- Liver and thyroid function
- Prostate health
- Stress markers

**This represents direct competitive entry into BodyCypher's core territory.** Pricing not yet disclosed, but expected to be "affordable" or "at-cost" for subscribers.

### Supplements
Supplement offerings are limited and clearly secondary to prescription products:
- Biotin Builder Gummies ($16/month)
- Melatonin Gummies
- Multivitamin Gummies (~$15)
- Protein shakes/bars (add-ons for weight loss program)

Available on hims.com, Amazon, Walmart, Target, and CVS. **Supplements appear to be cross-sell accessories, not a core revenue driver.**

### Technology and app features
- **MedMatch AI:** Proprietary system using machine learning to identify optimal treatments from 50M+ data points
- **Mobile app:** Prescription management, dose trackers, weight loss recipes, educational content, guided start videos
- **SOC2 compliant:** All patient data encrypted in transit and at rest
- **App ratings:** 4.8/5 (iOS, 72K reviews), 4.6/5 (Android, 13K reviews)

### Common praise themes
1. **Convenience and discretion** — 100% online, no embarrassing doctor visits, discreet packaging
2. **Fast prescription process** — Consultations within hours, 2–7 day delivery
3. **Product effectiveness** — Many success stories for ED, hair regrowth, weight loss
4. **Affordable pricing** — Often cheaper than traditional pharmacy/doctor visits
5. **Accessibility** — Access to treatments difficult to discuss in person

### Common complaint themes
1. **Subscription traps** — Auto-renewals without clear notification, unexpected charges of $700–$1,980
2. **Cancellation difficulties** — 48-hour window, orders can't be changed once "processing," no refund policy
3. **Customer service quality** — AI/bot responses only, no phone support, slow response times (3–5 days)
4. **Inconsistent effectiveness** — Hair loss treatments not working for some after 6+ months
5. **Side effects and medical oversight** — Reports of serious side effects (ED from finasteride, GI issues from GLP-1s), concerns about minimal screening

### Notable concerns
- **FTC investigation** over advertising practices and subscription cancellation difficulties
- **2,250+ BBB complaints closed in 12 months** alone
- **"No refund" policy** — All sales final, creating consumer risk on prepaid plans
- **Regulatory risk** — Compounded GLP-1s not FDA-approved; Novo Nordisk accused them of "illegal mass compounding"

The gap between app store ratings (4.6–4.8) and Trustpilot (3.0) suggests billing/cancellation issues drive negative reviews on open platforms.

## Business Model Assessment

### Revenue model
**90%+ subscription-based** with 30–360 day cadences. Average subscription prices range from $20–$120/month depending on treatment. The model prioritizes recurring revenue and long-term customer relationships over one-time transactions.

**Revenue breakdown (2024-2025):**
- Non-GLP-1 revenue: ~$1.2 billion (2024)
- GLP-1/weight loss: ~$225 million (2024) → projected $725 million (2025)
- Hers platform: ~30% of total revenue

### Unit economics

### Fulfillment infrastructure
Hims owns significant pharmacy infrastructure:
- **XeCare (Ohio):** 300,000–400,000 sq ft mail-order pharmacy
- **Apostrophe Pharmacy (Arizona):** 25,000 sq ft compounding facility
- **MedisourceRx (California):** 503B sterile compounding (acquired Sept 2024)
- **Peptide manufacturing facility:** Recently acquired for vertical integration

They also partner with **Alto Pharmacy** for same-day delivery in select markets.

### Three key strengths
1. **Vertical integration** — Owned pharmacy infrastructure enables 80%+ gross margins, personalized compounding, and supply control
2. **Subscription flywheel** — 85% retention, under-1-year CAC payback, and rising ARPU create predictable, high-quality revenue
3. **Category expansion playbook** — Successfully scaled from ED to hair to weight loss, demonstrating repeatable market entry

### Three key weaknesses
1. **Regulatory overhang** — Compounded GLP-1 business (~$725M projected) faces existential risk from FDA enforcement or legislative changes
2. **Customer service infrastructure** — High complaint volume (2,250+ BBB complaints/year) and subscription trap accusations damage trust
3. **Marketing dependency** — 46% of revenue spent on marketing; unclear if growth sustains if spending normalizes

## Key Takeaways for BodyCypher

### What to learn from / potentially copy
1. **Destigmatization messaging** — Hims mastered normalizing sensitive health topics; BodyCypher can apply this to preventive health and blood testing in Thailand's cultural context
2. **Subscription-first model** — 90%+ subscription revenue with 85% retention proves the model works for health; optimize for recurring relationships, not one-time purchases
3. **Entry-point pricing hooks** — "$4/dose" and "$199/month" capture attention; identify a compelling low-friction entry price for initial blood testing
4. **Celebrity/influencer authenticity** — Partners share personal health journeys, not just endorsements; recruit Thai wellness figures with genuine stories
5. **Aggressive content marketing** — $12.5M annual investment drives 1.4M monthly organic visitors; invest in SEO-optimized health education content for Thai market
6. **Vertical integration** — Owned pharmacy infrastructure enables 80%+ margins; consider owning or deeply integrating with lab partners
7. **Bundling and upsells** — 55% of subscribers have personalized solutions; design supplement bundles that complement blood test results

### What to avoid / do differently
1. **Subscription traps** — Complaints about hidden auto-renewals and cancellation difficulties damage brand; offer transparent, easy-to-cancel subscriptions
2. **No-refund policies** — "All sales final" creates consumer hostility; build trust with reasonable refund/guarantee policies
3. **Bot-only customer service** — No phone support fuels frustration; provide human support channels, especially for health-related questions
4. **Compounded medication regulatory risk** — Hims' GLP-1 business faces existential regulatory threat; build on FDA-approved or regulatory-compliant foundations
5. **Over-reliance on marketing spend** — 46% of revenue is unsustainable long-term; build organic growth and word-of-mouth earlier
6. **Minimal medical oversight perception** — Reports of 4-minute prescriptions without doctor contact hurt credibility; emphasize robust clinical involvement and personalization

### Gap/opportunity this reveals
**Hims is entering diagnostics but hasn't launched yet—and their model is telehealth-first, not diagnostics-first.** Their YourBio Health acquisition signals intent, but blood testing will be an add-on to support treatment personalization, not a core offering.

BodyCypher can differentiate by building **diagnostics as the foundation** rather than an afterthought:
- **Blood testing as the entry point** (not prescriptions)
- **Personalized supplements** (not medications requiring telehealth infrastructure)
- **Preventive/longevity framing** (not condition treatment)
- **Thailand market first** (no direct Hims competition yet)

The "test, then personalize" model is inverse to Hims' "consult, then prescribe" approach—potentially more defensible and requiring less regulatory complexity.

### Direct competitive threat level: **MEDIUM-LOW (currently), rising**

**Current:** Hims operates in US, UK, and now Canada/Europe—not Thailand. Their model requires state-by-state US medical licensing and wouldn't easily translate to Southeast Asia.

**Rising threat:** The YourBio Health acquisition shows strategic intent in diagnostics. If successful in the US, they could eventually license or expand the model internationally. Their 2030 target of $6.5B revenue suggests aggressive expansion plans.

**Key defense:** Move fast in Thailand/SEA, build local clinical partnerships, and establish the "blood testing + supplements" category before telehealth giants arrive.
