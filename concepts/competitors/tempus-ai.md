# Tempus AI: Competitive Intelligence Brief

**Tempus is a precision medicine company focused on oncology and AI-driven diagnostics—not a direct longevity/functional health competitor.** The company operates a B2B2C model selling primarily to healthcare providers and pharmaceutical companies, with patients as end-beneficiaries rather than direct customers. Publicly traded since June 2024 (NASDAQ: TEM), Tempus has raised ~$1.5B+ total capital and generated ~$1.27B in 2025 revenue, making it one of the largest players in clinical genomics.

## 3. Pricing Architecture

### Consumer/Patient Pricing

| Tier | Price | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Maximum Patient Guarantee** | $295 | No patient pays more regardless of test |
| **Tempus nP (PGx) Self-Pay** | $295 | Pharmacogenomic test for mental health |
| **Medicare Part B** | $0 | No out-of-pocket costs |
| **Medicaid/Government Insurance** | Typically $0 | No out-of-pocket costs |
| **Oncology with Financial Assistance** | ~$100 or less | For uninsured patients qualifying for FA |
| **Shipping (at-home kit)** | $7.99 | For direct-to-patient kits |

### Enterprise/B2B Pricing
- **Average Selling Price (oncology tests):** ~$1,600 per test (Q3 2025)
- **Major data licensing deals:**
  - GSK: $70M for 3-year partnership
  - AstraZeneca: $200M+ for data licensing and model development
  - 250+ biopharma partnerships generating ~$241.6M in Data & Services revenue (2024)

### Pricing Strategy Observations
- **Premium enterprise pricing** subsidizes patient accessibility
- **Insurance-first model** with robust financial assistance programs
- **Price-cap guarantee** removes patient anxiety about unexpected bills
- **Data monetization** provides significant high-margin revenue stream—tests generate data, data is licensed multiple times
- **No public pricing** for oncology tests (requires insurance check or FA application)
- **HSA/FSA eligible** — positions as preventive health expense

| xT CDx | 648 genes + MSI status | FFPE tumor tissue + blood/saliva | NGS |

| xM | MRD/disease monitoring | Blood | ctDNA |

**Algorithmic Tests:** HRD, IPS (immune profiling), TO (tumor origin), DPYD/UGT1A1 (toxicity risk), PurIST (pancreatic subtyping)

**Neuropsychiatry Tests:**

|------|----------|

## 8. Business Model Assessment

### Revenue Model
- **Genomics (65%):** Per-test diagnostics billed to insurance/institutions at ~$1,600 ASP
- **Data & Services (35%):** De-identified data licensing to pharma ($70M-$200M multi-year deals), Tempus Lens analytics platform, clinical trial matching (TIME Trial Program)
- **Emerging:** AI-enabled software subscriptions (Tempus One, Next, Pixel)

### Unit Economics (inferred)
- **Test ASP:** ~$1,600 (increasing)
- **Patient cap:** $295 max out-of-pocket
- **Data licensing:** High-margin recurring revenue (same data monetized multiple times)
- **Net Revenue Retention:** 140% indicates strong expansion within existing accounts
- **Gross Margin:** Improving with scale and data leverage

### Strengths
- **Massive data moat:** 40M+ records, 200+ petabytes creates network effects
- **Vertical integration:** Diagnostics → Data → AI Applications → Clinical Trial Matching
- **Pharma relationships:** 95% of top 20 oncology companies as partners
- **Clinical credibility:** FDA clearances, 65% of academic medical centers
- **Technology differentiation:** AI/ML capabilities, EHR integrations, generative AI
- **Multiple revenue streams:** Tests, data licensing, software, clinical trials

### Weaknesses
- **Concentration in oncology:** Limited diversification into wellness/longevity
- **B2B dependency:** Minimal direct consumer relationship
- **Billing/service complaints:** Consistent customer service issues per BBB
- **Internal culture:** Low employee satisfaction scores (Glassdoor 3.0/5)
- **Legal overhang:** Patent litigation and securities class action
- **Complex reimbursement:** Heavily dependent on insurance/Medicare coverage

## 9. Strategic Observations

### Notable Tactics & Approaches
- **"Virtuous cycle" strategy:** Tests generate data → Data trains AI → AI improves tests → Better tests attract more providers → More data. Self-reinforcing competitive advantage.
- **Price-cap guarantee:** "$295 max" messaging removes patient financial anxiety—unusual transparency in diagnostics.
- **EHR-first distribution:** Deep Epic integration makes ordering frictionless, embedding Tempus into clinical workflow.
- **Acquisitions for capability gaps:** Ambry (hereditary), Paige (pathology AI), Deep 6 (trial recruitment) show M&A-driven expansion strategy.
- **Pharma-subsidized scale:** Large data licensing deals effectively subsidize test volume growth.

### Gaps & Limitations (for longevity/functional health competitor)
- **No wellness/prevention positioning:** Entirely disease-focused (oncology, cardiology, neurology)—not longevity or optimization
- **No supplements or intervention products:** Pure diagnostics, no treatment or supplementation revenue
- **No subscription model:** One-time testing, no longitudinal monitoring relationship with consumers
- **No direct consumer relationship:** Physician-gated access limits consumer brand building
- **No lifestyle/functional biomarkers:** Tests focused on disease-relevant genes, not wellness optimization markers
- **Limited international presence:** Primarily US-focused; 40+ countries mentioned but emphasis is domestic

### Open Questions
1. Will Ambry acquisition bring hereditary/preventive positioning or remain disease-focused?
2. Can "olivia" app create direct consumer relationship, or will it remain physician-dependent?
3. How will May 2025 class action lawsuit and Spruce Point allegations affect company trajectory?
4. What is the path to profitability given aggressive growth investments?
5. Will Tempus expand into longevity/wellness biomarkers as precision medicine matures?
