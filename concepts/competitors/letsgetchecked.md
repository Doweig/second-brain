# LetsGetChecked: B2B Healthcare Platform Disguised as Consumer Testing Company

LetsGetChecked has quietly transformed from an at-home testing startup into a full-stack B2B healthcare infrastructure company. Following a **$165M Series F in January 2025** and its merger with Truepill to form Fuze Health, the company now serves **5,000+ enterprise clients** including Amazon, Salesforce, and major health plans—while maintaining its consumer brand as a strategic distribution channel. With **$500M in 2024 revenue** and over **10 million tests processed**, LetsGetChecked's pivot to B2B represents a significant strategic repositioning away from pure D2C.

## Positioning and messaging

### Exact homepage headlines

The website leads with **"Better care begins here"** followed by **"Delivering solutions to 5000+ organizations to help people live longer, happier lives."** The B2B orientation is immediate and prominent.

### Core value propositions (verbatim)

- **Mission statement**: "Our purpose is to empower people to live longer, happier lives."
- **Primary positioning**: "Patient-first healthcare designed for real lives. We remove barriers by delivering testing, consultations, and medication directly to people—wherever they are."
- **Brand identity**: "We are guided by science, empowered by technology, and passionate about caring for people."

### Target customer description

LetsGetChecked explicitly serves two audiences. **For individuals**: "We offer a range of tests to give you clear insights into your health and to help you take control of your wellbeing." **For enterprises**: "Closing gaps in care with virtual healthcare solutions. Serving employers, health plans, providers and the public sector."

### Key differentiators emphasized

The company emphasizes four distinct competitive advantages:

1. **End-to-end integrated care**: "What makes us unique is our ability to provide ongoing care beyond a test result. With our 360 model, our patients can seamlessly move from diagnostic testing to treatment within one platform."
2. **Owned lab infrastructure**: CLIA-certified, CAP-accredited labs in California and Dublin enable "big insights from small samples" through micro-quantity processing optimization.
3. **Proprietary technology platform (Halo)**: Custom-built EMR linking sample collection, laboratory, clinical, and pharmacy services—described as "interoperable EMR built to support both individuals and organizations."
4. **Genomics capability**: Following the 2022 Veritas Genetics acquisition, they offer direct-to-home genetic testing providing "a life-long map of potential medical conditions predicted from one single sample."

## Product and service details

### Testing scope and methodology

LetsGetChecked offers **30+ diagnostic tests** across five categories: Wellness/General Health, Women's Health, Men's Health, Sexual Health, and COVID-19. The company measures **50+ biomarkers** and has detected nearly **250,000 abnormal results**.

**Sample collection methods** include finger-prick blood (most tests), urine, stool (colon cancer FIT), saliva (cortisol, PCOS), and swabs (STI tests). The company has transitioned from dried blood spot to "wet" samples in tubes for improved accuracy.

### Turnaround time and processing

Results arrive in **2-5 business days** from lab receipt, with many users reporting results within 24-48 hours. Labs operate under CLIA certification and CAP accreditation—the same standards used by hospitals. Manufacturing of sample collection kits occurs in-house at an **ISO 13485 certified facility**.

### Supplement offering

**None**. LetsGetChecked does not sell supplements—no own brand, no marketplace, no recommendations for purchase. Their model focuses purely on testing with clinical guidance on addressing deficiencies through healthcare providers.

### App availability and features

Available on both **iOS (4.9/5 stars, 9,400+ ratings)** and **Android (4.88/5 stars, 9,300+ ratings)**. Key features include:
- Test kit ordering and activation via barcode scanning
- Secure results dashboard with historical tracking
- Virtual consultations through secure messaging
- Prescription management and shipment tracking
- Integration with Apple Health and Garmin wearables
- Biometric authentication (Face ID/Touch ID)
- Digital recommender tool for test selection

### Clinical involvement model

All orders and results are reviewed by **board-certified physicians**. The clinical team provides:
- **Nurse follow-up**: Free consultations for abnormal results
- **Telehealth**: $49 text-based consultations (available in 40 US states)
- **Prescription services**: Through owned affiliate pharmacy (acquired 2021) with home delivery or local pharmacy pickup
- **24/7 pharmacy support**: Toll-free phone line

Prescriptions available include ED medications, birth control, and STD treatments. Notably excluded: controlled substances, hormone replacement therapy, and sleep medications.

### Positive review themes

Customers consistently praise **convenience** (home testing eliminates clinic visits), **speed** (results typically 2-5 days), **clear instructions**, **discreet packaging**, and **nurse follow-up** for abnormal results. On Trustpilot, **82% of reviews are 5-star**.

### Negative review themes

Five pain points recur across platforms:
1. **Blood collection difficulty**: Finger-prick tests challenging for many users; "insufficient sample" rejections common despite following instructions
2. **Kit expiration policies**: Short expiration dates with no refund or replacement options
3. **Refund and customer service issues**: Difficulty obtaining refunds; slow response times reported
4. **Telehealth limitations**: $49 consultations sometimes result in "go see someone in person"
5. **Shipping inconsistencies**: Delays despite expedited shipping in some cases

### BBB rating discrepancy

The stark contrast between **4.5-4.9 star ratings** on consumer platforms and a **D- BBB rating** reveals a complaint resolution gap. The BBB cites "failure to respond to 21 complaint(s) filed against business." Common BBB complaints involve refund disputes, expired kit policies, and processing failures. This suggests that while most customer experiences are positive, the company struggles with escalated complaint resolution.

## Business model assessment

### Revenue model structure

LetsGetChecked operates a **hybrid D2C and B2B model**, with B2B now dominant. Revenue streams include:
- **D2C test sales**: $89-$249 per test
- **Subscription revenue**: Recurring testing at 15-30% discounts
- **Telehealth consultations**: $49 per visit
- **B2B enterprise contracts**: Health plans, employers, providers (5,000+ organizations)
- **Pharmacy services**: Prescription fulfillment through owned pharmacy infrastructure

The 2024 Truepill acquisition and 2025 Alto Pharmacy addition signal aggressive vertical integration into pharmacy services.

### Strategic strengths

1. **Full-stack healthcare infrastructure**: Owns labs, manufacturing, pharmacy, and technology platform—rare vertical integration
2. **Enterprise distribution**: 5,000+ B2B clients provide recurring revenue and scale
3. **Clinical credibility**: CLIA/CAP-accredited labs, physician oversight, nurse support
4. **Genomics positioning**: Veritas acquisition enables whole-genome sequencing capability
5. **Strong consumer app ratings**: 4.9/5 stars indicates solid user experience
6. **Geographic reach**: US, UK, Canada, Europe, UAE operations

### Strategic weaknesses and gaps

1. **Valuation compression**: Down from $1B (2021) to ~$525M combined entity post-merger, reflecting market realities
2. **No supplement revenue stream**: Missing ancillary revenue opportunity competitors leverage
3. **Customer service gaps**: D- BBB rating and complaint resolution issues
4. **No video telehealth**: Text-only consultations limit care complexity
5. **Blood collection UX**: Finger-prick difficulty is consistent customer pain point
6. **Insurance exclusion**: Not accepting insurance creates friction and limits accessibility

## Strategic observations

### Notable tactical moves

**The pivot to B2B represents a fundamental strategic reorientation.** The company's messaging now leads with "5,000+ organizations" before consumer value propositions. The Truepill and Alto Pharmacy acquisitions create a closed-loop testing-to-treatment ecosystem specifically designed for enterprise clients managing population health.

**Genomics ambitions are underexplored in current positioning.** Despite acquiring Veritas Genetics in 2022 and employing a Chief Genetics Officer, genomics receives minimal emphasis in current marketing—suggesting either delayed integration or strategic sequencing.

### Open questions for further investigation

- **Leadership transition impact**: Peter Foley stepped down as CEO in April 2025—who has replaced him and what does this signal?
- **Fuze Health brand architecture**: How will the LetsGetChecked consumer brand coexist with the Fuze Health enterprise platform?
- **Path to profitability**: Despite $500M revenue, profitability status is unclear; both LetsGetChecked and Truepill were reportedly "burning cash" at merger
- **Competitive response**: How are Everlywell, Quest Diagnostics, and Labcorp responding to LetsGetChecked's B2B push?
- **Genomics roadmap**: When and how will whole-genome sequencing be commercialized at scale?

### Key competitive intelligence takeaways

LetsGetChecked is no longer primarily a consumer at-home testing company—it's a **B2B healthcare infrastructure platform** with testing, telehealth, and pharmacy capabilities serving enterprise clients. The consumer brand now appears to function partly as a distribution channel and brand awareness driver for the enterprise business. This positioning shift, combined with aggressive M&A (Truepill, Alto, Veritas), suggests a strategy to become the outsourced healthcare infrastructure layer for health plans, employers, and providers—competing less with consumer testing brands and more with traditional healthcare service companies.
