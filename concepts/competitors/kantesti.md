# Kantesti: AI Blood Test Interpreter, Not a Testing Company

**Kantesti is a German-based AI platform that interprets existing blood test results—not a blood testing service itself.** Users upload lab results (PDFs or images) and receive AI-generated health reports within 60 seconds. The company claims **2 million+ users across 127 countries** and operates a freemium business model with premium tiers starting at €6.24 per report. Founded in May 2020 by Julian Emirhan Bulut in Cologne, Germany, Kantesti has secured technology partnerships with Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Google Cloud, though **no external funding has been publicly disclosed**.

---

## Business model: Upload-based interpretation only

Kantesti's fundamental offering differs from blood testing companies like Everlywell or Thriva. The platform does **not collect blood samples, sell test kits, operate labs, or maintain clinic partnerships**. Instead, users upload existing lab results from any source—LabCorp, Quest Diagnostics, Synlab, or hospital systems—and receive AI-powered interpretation.

The company's value proposition centers on three claims:
- **"Medical-Grade AI Blood Test Analyzer"** — the primary homepage headline
- **60-second analysis turnaround** — consistently emphasized
- **99.84% clinical accuracy** — though accuracy claims range from 85% to 99.84% depending on pricing tier

Key product capabilities include support for **10,000+ laboratory formats**, **75+ languages**, and compatibility with PDF, JPG, and PNG file uploads. Reports span **10-45 pages** with biomarker breakdowns, health scores, risk indicators, and lifestyle recommendations. The platform offers web access, iOS and Android mobile apps, and a Chrome browser extension.

**What Kantesti does NOT offer:** physical blood test kits, at-home sample collection, partner lab networks for blood draws, doctor consultations, telehealth services, or physical supplements.

## Pricing architecture: Freemium with four tiers

Kantesti operates a clear freemium model with aggressive promotional discounting (currently 60% off with "WINTER60" code):

| Tier | Price | Key Inclusions |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Free** | €0 | 1 basic report, 85% accuracy AI, PDF output |
| **Single Report** | €6.24 (discounted from ~€15.60) | One comprehensive analysis, 92% accuracy AI |
| **Annual Package** | €59.90/year (~€4.98/month) | 12 reports, AI nutrition plans, supplement recommendations, family sharing (3 profiles), 98% accuracy AI |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Unlimited analysis, EMR/EHR integration, white-label platform, API access, 99.5% AI |

The **entry point is completely free** (one basic report), making user acquisition frictionless. The Annual Package is positioned as "Best Value" and represents the primary monetization target. Enterprise offerings include API access and white-label capabilities for B2B integrations with labs, clinics, and insurance providers.

**Upsell features** restricted to Annual/Enterprise tiers include AI Nutrition Diet Plans, Smart Supplement Recommendations, Advanced Blood Test Comparison, batch upload (15-20 reports simultaneously), and predictive health modeling.

## Product claims show notable inconsistencies

Several core product claims vary significantly across Kantesti's own website:

| Metric | Stated Values | Assessment |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| Neural network parameters | 2.38 trillion / 2.78 trillion | Conflicting claims |
| AI accuracy | 85% / 92% / 98.7% / 99.84% | Varies by tier and page |
| AI "IQ" | 110 / 120 / 138 / 145 / 152 / 156 | Marketing construct varies |
| Biomarkers supported | 100+ / 127+ / 300+ / 450+ / 1,000+ | Wide range |
| Users | 50,000+ / 2M+ / 10M+ | Significant variation |

The company's AI claims are exceptionally aggressive. A **"2.78 trillion parameter neural network"** would exceed GPT-4's estimated 1.7 trillion parameters—an extraordinary claim for a startup without disclosed funding. Accuracy claims reference "internal validation studies" with results "pending peer review," meaning **no independent third-party verification exists**.

**Critical contradiction:** The homepage prominently states "CE-Marked Medical Device," while the medical disclaimer explicitly clarifies Kantesti "is NOT a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." This messaging inconsistency presents regulatory and trust concerns.

## Medical involvement is advisory, not individual review

Kantesti employs a **full-time Chief Medical Officer** (Prof. Dr. Thomas Klein, MD, board-certified clinical hematologist) and maintains a **12-physician medical advisory board**. Blog content is attributed to Dr. Klein as "Physician Reviewed" and "Written & Medically Reviewed."

However, **no individual doctor reviews user results**. The service is entirely AI-driven—users receive automated reports without physician consultation or telehealth follow-up. The medical team's role appears limited to:
- AI model training and validation oversight
- Content review and medical accuracy
- Clinical methodology development

The company claims validation performance by test category (e.g., 99.3% sensitivity for Complete Blood Count), but these metrics come from "internal validation studies" rather than published peer-reviewed research. Clinical validation status is listed as **"Peer Review Pending."**

## Conclusion: High marketing claims, limited verification

Kantesti occupies a distinct niche as a **blood test interpretation service** rather than a testing company, differentiating it from competitors who sell physical test kits. The **freemium model with €6.24 entry pricing** creates low friction for user acquisition, while the claimed **2M+ user base** and **127-country reach** suggest meaningful scale if accurate.

However, several factors warrant caution:

- **Extraordinary AI claims** (2.78 trillion parameters, 99.84% accuracy) lack independent verification
- **Conflicting messaging** about medical device status creates regulatory ambiguity
- **Only 3 independent reviews** exist despite claiming millions of users
- **Enterprise partnerships** (NHS, major insurers) cannot be corroborated
- **No disclosed funding** for a company making infrastructure-heavy AI claims
- **Cryptocurrency token launch** may indicate alternative financing needs

The company demonstrates legitimate infrastructure (German headquarters, technology partnerships, professional website) and a coherent business model. The SEO-focused content strategy and medical advisory board add credibility. However, **the gap between marketing claims and verifiable evidence is significant**, and potential customers or partners should request independent validation of accuracy claims and enterprise references.
