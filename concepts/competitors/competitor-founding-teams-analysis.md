## Pattern Analysis

### Is the doctor a co-founder or employee?

| Company | Doctor as Co-Founder? | Result |
|---------|----------------------|--------|
| Function Health | ✅ Yes (Mark Hyman, CMO) | $2.5B. Hyman's brand IS the growth engine. |
| [[superpower|Superpower]] | ❌ No — hired clinicians | $51M raised. Pure tech/business team. |
| Bioniq | ✅ Yes (Karuzin, Medical Director) | $82M val, 250K members. Doctor built core IP. |
| Everlab | ✅ Yes (Dr. Steven Lu, CMO) | $15M seed. Explicitly said they needed the clinician as co-founder. |
| Mito Health | ✅ Yes (Dr. Ryan Ware) | YC S24. Doctor gives credibility for YC/investors. |

**Verdict: 4 out of 5 have the doctor as co-founder.** Only Superpower doesn't — and they compensated with $51M in capital to hire top clinicians. At [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]]'s stage and budget, having the doctor as co-founder is the right call. You can't buy that credibility.

### The three core functions every funded team covers

1. **CEO / Business & Fundraising** — Sells the vision. Raises money. Closes partnerships.
2. **CTO / Product & Tech** — Builds the product. Owns the stack.
3. **CMO / Medical** — Provides clinical credibility. Reviews outputs. Content/brand face (especially in Asia where trust = doctor's face).

The 4th function that keeps appearing (Everlab, Function) is **Growth/Brand/Ops** — but it's the one most commonly handled by the CEO early-stage or added as co-founder #4.

### Why the 3rd seat is Business/Growth, not "marketing"

Your intuition is right but the framing matters. You don't need a "marketing person" — you need someone who can:
- **Sell** (close customers, close partnerships, close investors)
- **Grow** (brand, content strategy, community, distribution)
- **Operate** (hiring, finance, legal oversight, vendor management)
- **Raise** (investor relationships, pitch, negotiate terms)

This is the classic CEO/COO hybrid. In a 3-person founding team, this person is usually the CEO. You'd be CTO.
