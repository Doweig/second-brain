# Function Health — Competitive Analysis

**Direct-to-consumer preventive health testing leader valued at $2.5 billion** has captured 200,000+ paying members with a compelling value proposition: 160+ lab tests for $365/year—what they claim would cost $15,000 elsewhere. Backed by $350M in funding and celebrity investors from Matt Damon to Magic Johnson, Function has positioned itself as the "anti-Theranos"—partnering exclusively with [[quest-diagnostics|Quest Diagnostics]] rather than developing proprietary lab technology. Their recent acquisition of MRI startup [[ezra|Ezra]] signals expansion beyond blood testing into comprehensive preventive diagnostics.

## Positioning and messaging

### Exact homepage headline (verbatim)
> "Your advanced health check"

### How they describe themselves (exact quotes)
> "It's time you own your health. Function includes 160+ lab tests and personalized protocols for instant action. Tracked over time in one secure place."

> "Function was founded with a singular focus: empower you to live 100 healthy years."

> "What could cost you $15,000 is $499" (now $365)

### Target customer description
Health-conscious consumers seeking proactive, preventive care—people who "feel good" but want early detection. Their language targets those frustrated with reactive healthcare: "the delays, the dismissals, the reactive model that waits for symptoms." Heavy emphasis on empowerment language: "own your health," "more powerful patient," "take control." Appeals specifically to longevity-minded audiences with their "100 healthy years" tagline.

### Key differentiators
1. **Volume of testing**: 160+ biomarkers annually vs. 20-30 in typical physicals ("5x more tests than the average physical")
2. **Price transparency**: Single $365/year membership with no insurance involvement, no deductibles, no surprise bills
3. **Medical credibility**: High-profile advisory board (Harvard, Cleveland Clinic, Stanford, Memorial Sloan Kettering)
4. **Lab legitimacy**: Quest Diagnostics partnership only—explicitly no at-home kits or "startup labs"
5. **Longitudinal tracking**: Designed for lifetime monitoring, not one-time testing
6. **Integrated imaging**: Ezra acquisition adds $499 full-body MRI scans

### Positioning statement interpretation
Function positions itself as the democratization of concierge medicine—executive health programs that cost $5,000-$15,000 made accessible at ~$1/day. They explicitly reject the comparison to consumer testing startups by emphasizing established lab partnerships and physician oversight. Their brand operates at the intersection of longevity science, biohacking culture, and mainstream preventive healthcare.

## Pricing architecture

### All tiers with prices and inclusions

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| **Single Membership** | **$365/year** (reduced from $499 in Nov 2025) | 160+ lab tests annually (100+ in Annual Test, 60+ in Mid-Year Test), clinician review, written summary, personalized action plan, food guide, supplement recommendations, lifetime tracking |

**Function operates a single-tier model.** Per their FAQ: "Are there different tiers of membership? No. There's just one Function membership."

### Add-on services (member-only pricing)

| Add-On | Price | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Full-body MRI | $499 | 22-minute scan via Ezra; head, neck, abdomen, pelvis |
| [[grail|GRAIL]] Galleri Test | ~$899 (estimated) | Multi-cancer early detection, 50+ cancer types |
| MRI with Spine | Not disclosed | ~47 minutes, includes spinal assessment |
| Heart & Lungs CT | Not disclosed | ~3-minute low-dose CT with CAC score |
| Pregnancy Testing | ~$3 | Basic panel |
| Sexual health, Lyme, allergies, heavy metals | Varies | Various specialty panels |

### Entry point and hook
The **$365/year** membership (~$1/day) serves as the primary entry point. The waitlist creates artificial scarcity and social proof—though it can be bypassed with referral codes. The "$15,000 value for $365" framing anchors the perceived value proposition.

### Upsell structure
Add-on tests are positioned as premium options available "when you need them." The GRAIL cancer test and MRI scans represent significant upsell opportunities at **$499-$899 per add-on**. Users report "aggressive upselling" during the scheduling process.

### Pricing strategy observations
The November 2025 price reduction from $499 to $365 suggests either competitive pressure, a land-grab strategy enabled by Series B funding, or both. HSA/FSA eligibility broadens accessibility. The lack of tiered pricing simplifies the purchase decision but may leave money on the table from users willing to pay more for premium features.

## Product and service details

### Testing specifications

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Biomarkers tested** | 160+ annually (100+ in Annual Test, 60+ in Mid-Year Test) |
| **Testing method** | In-person blood draw at Quest Diagnostics locations (2,000+ sites); no at-home kits |
| **Turnaround time** | Initial results within days; complete results in 3-4 weeks; clinician summary added 1-2 weeks after |
| **Testing frequency** | 2x per year (annual + mid-year); on-demand retesting available for additional cost |
| **Lab visits required** | Typically 1 visit (~15 min); some members require 2 visits (menstrual cycle timing or Arizona regulations) |
| **Availability** | 48 states (not Rhode Island, Hawaii, or Puerto Rico); NY and NJ have additional Quest fees |

### Biomarker categories covered
Heart & metabolic (18 markers), hormones & thyroid (16 markers), cancer & silent risks (34 markers), aging (26 markers), mental health & focus (36 markers), nutrients, toxins (mercury, lead), autoimmunity, inflammation.

### Supplements model
**Recommendations only**—no proprietary supplement brand or marketplace. Function provides personalized supplement recommendations based on test results. However, CMO Mark Hyman separately sells ~500 supplement products through drhyman.com, creating a potential conflict of interest noted by critics.

### Technology and app

| Feature | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **App availability** | iOS and Android |
| **Dashboard** | Web and mobile; user-friendly interface with clear explanations for each biomarker |
| **AI claims** | AI chatbot launched November 2025 for personalized health Q&A; "Medical Intelligence Lab" announced |
| **Integrations** | Apple Health, Garmin, Fitbit, Oura, Peloton, Withings, iFit |
| **Data export** | PDF downloads available; no consolidated export (criticized by users) |
| **Anthropic partnership** | January 2026 partnership for Claude Healthcare connector |

### Doctor and clinical involvement
Function operates as a **"healthcare technology company, not a laboratory or medical provider."** All lab services provided by Quest Diagnostics; medical oversight provided by independent clinicians.

Clinical model:
- **Clinician review**: Every result is reviewed by clinicians who examine health history and flag issues
- **Written summaries**: Personalized clinician notes with action items
- **Critical results protocol**: Healthcare provider calls immediately for critical findings
- **Medical content**: Created by Function's medical team including CMO Mark Hyman, grounded in peer-reviewed studies from NIH, JAMA, NEJM
- **Not a doctor replacement**: Explicitly positioned as making users "more powerful patients" rather than replacing physician relationships

## Business model assessment

### Revenue model
- **Core**: Annual membership subscription ($365/year)
- **Ancillary**: Add-on premium tests (GRAIL ~$899, MRI $499, specialty panels)
- **Enterprise**: "Function for Work" B2B offering for employers
- **Estimated metrics** (per Sacra, Feb 2025): ~$100M ARR, ~$39/subscriber annually from add-ons, 450% YoY growth

### Unit economics (inferred)
With Quest Diagnostics wholesale pricing likely around $100-150 per comprehensive panel, gross margins on the $365 membership could be **50-60%**. Add-on tests at $499-899 likely carry higher margins. The asset-light model (no proprietary labs, outsourced blood draws, clinician reviews likely contracted) enables operational leverage.

### Strengths
1. **Distribution moat**: 200K+ members, 500K+ waitlist, celebrity investor network creates organic marketing machine
2. **Pricing power**: Single transparent price undercuts traditional healthcare complexity; HSA/FSA eligible
3. **Platform expansion**: Ezra acquisition enables move from blood testing to comprehensive diagnostics (imaging, potentially genomics)

### Weaknesses
1. **Customer service deficits**: No phone support and difficult cancellations generate negative word-of-mouth in target communities
2. **Conflict of interest concerns**: CMO Mark Hyman's separate supplement business creates credibility questions
3. **Regulatory risk**: DTC testing faces potential scrutiny; state-by-state variation (NY/NJ already have additional fees)

## Strategic observations

### Notable tactics and approaches
- **"Anti-Theranos" positioning**: Explicit rejection of proprietary lab technology in favor of established Quest partnership creates trust
- **Celebrity capital leverage**: Investor list functions as implicit endorsement network driving organic awareness
- **Artificial scarcity**: Waitlist creates FOMO while referral codes provide bypass mechanism for growth
- **Vertical integration**: Ezra acquisition signals ambition beyond blood testing into full-body preventive diagnostics
- **Price reduction strategy**: $499→$365 cut suggests prioritizing member acquisition over near-term revenue

### Gaps and limitations
- **No supplement marketplace**: Unlike competitors ([[insidetracker|InsideTracker]], [[viome|Viome]]), Function provides recommendations only—missing revenue opportunity
- **Limited clinical integration**: Positioned as complement to physicians but no direct EHR integration or care coordination
- **Customer support infrastructure**: Rapid growth has outpaced support capabilities, creating friction
- **Geographic limitations**: Excludes 3 states/territories; NY/NJ have added costs

### Open questions
- How will they monetize the AI/Medical Intelligence Lab capabilities?
- What is the path to profitability given the price reduction and heavy growth investment?
- How will they differentiate as competitors ([[prenuvo|Prenuvo]], [[neko-health|Neko Health]], InsideTracker) expand offerings?
- Will Mark Hyman's supplement business and friendship with RFK Jr. create regulatory/PR challenges?
- What is the retention rate after first-year membership?
