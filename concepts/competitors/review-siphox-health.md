# Product Review: SiPhox Health

*Reviewer: Doweig | Date: February 11, 2026 | Plan used: Free (SAI / SiPhox AI trial)*

---

## Section 1: Product identity

**Product name:** SiPhox Health
**Website:** https://app.siphoxhealth.com/portal/dashboard/CUSTBT9TX8H
**Price I paid (or free tier):** Free - they allowed trying the new AI feature ("SAI" / SiPhox AI)
**What data I submitted:** Multiple PDFs including [[bdms-wellness|BDMS]] report from December 2024 (most extensive) and reports from PathLab and Enhance
**How many biomarkers did it recognize from my upload:** Variable - newer PDFs (PathLab, Enhance) were recognized better than the older BDMS multi-page reports
**Date of review:** February 11, 2026

---

## Section 2: Onboarding (first 5 minutes)

**How did I submit my data?**
PDF upload. The platform allowed uploading multiple PDFs, which was a positive surprise in terms of capacity.

**What personal info did they ask for?**
Don't recall specifics - onboarding was pretty straightforward. Just uploaded PDFs, wasn't asked many questions.

**How long did onboarding take?**
Quick, but the upload process itself was problematic (see below).

**Did it recognize my lab format?**
Mixed results. The older BDMS files with multiple data pages were struggling to be recognized. The more recent reports from PathLab and Enhance were recognized much better. The BDMS December report, despite being the most extensive, had the most issues.

**First impression (gut reaction):**
The upload itself was buggy. Sometimes clicking multiple times would create new reports instead of updating data, so I ended up with the same PDF uploaded 10 times. Had to manually remove duplicates. Finicky PDF import feature - some PDFs work better than others. A bit messy start.

---

## Section 3: Dashboard / global view

**What is the main screen showing me?**
The UI has three sections, all competing for attention: a left sidebar with navigation and features, the main content area in the center with biomarker summaries, and a right panel with the AI chat featuring pre-made messages. Even the AI chat section is busy with suggested prompts. Your screen is just full of stuff.

There are effectively two dashboard views: the main Dashboard page (organ scores) and the My Results page (more detailed, includes biological age).

**Do they give a single global score?**
Not a single unified score, but they show a biological age on the My Results page. Mine showed 1 year younger than actual age. Notably, the biological age is NOT on the main dashboard - only on the My Results page, which feels like a miss.

**Do they give category or organ-level scores?**
Yes, six organ/system categories:

| Category/Organ | Score | Scale | My reaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Heart Health | 72% | Percentage | Seems reasonable given my LDL |
| Metabolic Health | 80% | Percentage | Okay |
| Kidney Health | Not noted | Percentage | - |
| Liver Health | Not noted | Percentage | - |
| Thyroid Health | Not noted | Percentage | - |
| Blood Health | Not noted | Percentage | - |

**Color coding or visual system:**
Color-coded system with multiple colors - actually too many colors. The action plan in particular uses a lot of colors that make things harder to parse, not easier.

**What stands out visually?**
The sheer amount of information. Three busy panels on every screen. The dashboard tries to mix call-to-action elements with the biomarker summary per organ, which creates visual clutter.

**What's missing from this view?**
Biological age should be on the main dashboard, not buried in My Results. The dashboard mixes promotional/CTA elements with actual health data, which dilutes the core content.

## Section 4: Biomarker detail view

**My actual value:** Not noted (above optimal range)
**Their reference range:**
- Lab Range: Out of range (low) < 0 mg/dL | In range < 130 mg/dL | Out of range (high) > 130 mg/dL
**Do they show an optimal range (separate from lab range)?** Yes - SiPhox Ranges:
- Optimal Range: 40-90 mg/dL

**Recommendations provided:**

- Avoid trans fat (to lower LDL)
- Increase cardio

## Section 5: Action plan / recommendations

**Do they have a synthesized action plan?**
Yes - they regroup all the tips from individual biomarkers into a single action plan page.

**How is the action plan organized?**
Not well organized. 13 action blocks displayed without ranking, grouping, or prioritization. Multiple display modes available (per category, list, or simple/table view), which adds flexibility but also complexity.

**Action plan contents:**
13 recommended actions in total. Each presented as a separate block with associated colors and biomarker connections. Too much to digest in one sitting.

**Is it cross-referenced across biomarkers?**
Partially - the actions reference which biomarkers they relate to, but the presentation doesn't synthesize them into a coherent protocol. It's essentially a collection of per-biomarker tips regrouped on one page.

**Prioritization:**
No. The 13 actions are not ranked or sequenced. No "start here" or "most important" signal.

**Specificity rating (1-5):**
**My rating:** 2/5 - Recommendations like "avoid trans fat" and "increase cardio" are closer to generic advice than specific protocols. No dosing, no quantities, no timelines.

**Personalization rating (1-5):**
**My rating:** 2/5 - Actions reference my specific report values, but the recommendations themselves feel like they could apply to anyone with similar flags. Not tailored to my full profile.

Yes. They have a timeline feature where you can roll back to previous reports and see the dashboard state at each point. Interesting approach.

**Can I upload older lab results to build history?**
Yes - the platform accepts multiple PDF uploads.

**Do they show before/after comparisons?**
No direct before/after overlay comparison.

## Section 10: Overall assessment

**What did this product do better than anything I've seen?**
The multi-range system per biomarker. Having both lab reference ranges (with multiple thresholds) AND SiPhox optimal/good/fair ranges shows genuine research depth. Plus the external source links per biomarker (CDC, NIH, Peter Attia) add real credibility. They've clearly done their homework on the clinical and research side.

**What was the biggest disappointment or gap?**
The UI/UX undermines everything. Too busy, too cluttered, three competing panels on every screen. The action plan with 13 unranked, ungrouped blocks is overwhelming. Great research, poor presentation.

**Would I pay for this? Why or why not?**
Hesitant. The underlying data and research quality is strong, but the experience of using it is too cluttered to feel valuable. The action plan doesn't synthesize well enough to drive real behavior change.

**Would I recommend this to a friend?**
Only to someone who's already comfortable interpreting health data and wants a second-opinion tool. Not to someone looking for clear guidance on what to do.

**What should BodyCypher steal from this product?**
1. The multi-tier range system (lab ranges + optimal ranges) - this is excellent and well-researched
2. External source links per biomarker (linking to CDC, NIH, physician content) builds trust
3. The eGFR-style visual explanations with diagrams - apply this depth to every biomarker
4. The timeline/rollback concept for historical reports (but improve it into actual trend graphs)

**What should BodyCypher explicitly NOT copy?**
1. The three-panel layout - too busy, too much competing for attention
2. The action plan as a flat list of 13 unranked blocks - needs synthesis, prioritization, and grouping
3. Mixing CTAs/promotional content into the health dashboard
4. The buggy PDF upload experience - needs to be rock-solid since it's the first touchpoint

**How does this compare to the BodyCypher vision?**
SiPhox validates that the range system and sourcing matter - they've done the research layer well. But the action plan, which is [[bodycypher|BodyCypher]]'s core differentiator, is weak here. Their 13 unranked actions confirm the customer sentiment finding that "lists of per-biomarker tips" are not what users want. BodyCypher's vision of a synthesized, prioritized, doctor-reviewed action plan would be meaningfully better than what SiPhox offers. The interpretation gap that SiPhox partially closes with good explanations remains wide open on the "what to actually do" side.

**Overall rating (1-10):**
5/10 - Strong research foundation, weak execution on presentation and actionability.
