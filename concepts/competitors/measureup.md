# MeasureUp Australia: Competitive Intelligence Brief

MeasureUp is a body composition and performance testing company—not a blood biomarker service. Since 2006, they've built Australia's leading DEXA scanning business with **11-14 mobile labs**, **2,500+ medical centre partnerships**, and an estimated **$5.4 million annual revenue**. Their model combines direct-to-consumer clinics with a B2B2C healthcare distribution strategy via "The Bone Bus" that bulk-bills Medicare for bone density scans. For a Thailand-based longevity startup focused on blood testing and supplements, MeasureUp represents an adjacent competitor in the body composition measurement space, not a direct competitor in blood biomarkers.

RMR test pricing not listed separately but is health-insurance claimable (item number 202). No subscription model exists—all purchases are one-time transactions.

## Product and service details

### Core services

| Service | What It Measures | Method | Duration |
|---------|------------------|--------|----------|
| **DEXA Body Composition** | Muscle mass, fat mass (regional), body fat %, visceral fat (VAT), bone mineral density | Medical-grade DEXA scanner | 3 min scan, 15-30 min appointment |
| **VO2 Max Test** | Maximum oxygen consumption, cardiovascular fitness, training zones | Treadmill/Wattbike with gas analyzer mask | ~20 min test, 60 min appointment |
| **Resting Metabolic Rate** | Daily caloric expenditure at rest | Indirect calorimetry | Not specified |
| **Grip Strength** | Functional strength, frailty indicator | Dynamometer | Quick assessment |

### What they measure vs. what they don't

MeasureUp measures **physical composition markers** (body fat, muscle mass, bone density, VO2 max, metabolic rate) but does **NOT** offer blood biomarker testing. There are no tests for cholesterol, glucose, hormones, inflammation markers, vitamin levels, or any blood-based health indicators. This represents a significant service gap for consumers seeking comprehensive metabolic health assessment.

### Testing model
- **In-clinic**: Sydney (Pitt Street) and Brisbane (Newstead) locations with same-day results
- **Mobile labs**: 11-14 DEXA-equipped vans visit gyms, workplaces, and medical centres
- **No at-home testing** available
- **Turnaround**: Same-day for clinic visits; 48 hours for mobile scans

### Supplement offering
**None.** MeasureUp does not sell supplements, recommend specific products, or operate a marketplace. Reports include macronutrient recommendations only.

### App and digital experience
- **iOS App**: "MeasureUp Scan App" — view results, download DEXA reports. Requires iOS 9.3+. User reviews indicate crashing issues when opening reports.
- **Android App**: Not found — likely no native Android app exists
- **Web Portal**: scans.measureup.com.au for accessing results online
- **AI/personalization**: No explicit AI claims. Personalization delivered through human Exercise Physiologist consultations.

### Clinical involvement model
Results are reviewed and explained by **Accredited Exercise Physiologists** (university-qualified). Dr. Jarrod Meerkin (PhD) provides clinical oversight. No doctors on staff for medical consultations—they partner with GPs and refer.

## Customer Acquisition Strategy

### Partnership-driven growth is the dominant channel

MeasureUp's acquisition model relies heavily on B2B2C distribution rather than direct-to-consumer marketing. **The Bone Bus** brand operates **2,500+ medical centre partnerships** across Australia, parking mobile DEXA units at GP practices to bulk-bill Medicare for bone density scans (for patients 70+). This creates a healthcare distribution moat.

### Channel breakdown

1. **Medical centre partnerships (The Bone Bus)**: 2,500+ practices; Medicare bulk-billing for eligible patients; reports sent via HealthLink to GP systems; no cost for practices to host
2. **Gym partnerships**: Mobile labs visit gyms; 10-minute scan appointments; supports gym challenges and challenges
3. **Corporate wellness**: Mobile labs visit workplaces; positioned as reducing absenteeism and WorkCover claims
4. **Fixed clinics**: Sydney CBD and Brisbane for direct consumers
5. **Referral program**: Partners (fitness pros, health businesses, influencers) receive unique discount codes; clients get 10%+ off; volume-based incentives available

### Content strategy
The blog publishes **1-2 posts monthly** focused on longevity themes: resistance training and aging, sarcopenia, protein recommendations, bone health, creatine, sleep. Content aligns with Peter Attia's "Outlive" positioning. Categories include: Client of the Month, Exercise Programs, News, Newsletters, Recipes.

### Digital advertising infrastructure
- **Facebook Pixel** confirmed on website (ID: 2983534228494968) indicating Facebook/Instagram retargeting capability
- **Newsletter signup** prominently featured for email marketing
- Search advertising presence not verified

### Celebrity social proof (not formal influencer program)
Historical high-profile clients used for credibility: **Hugh Jackman, Samantha Stosur** (US Open winner), **Casey Dellacqua**, **Wallabies**, multiple NRL teams (Roosters, Eels, Tigers, Sea Eagles, Bulldogs). No evidence of formal ambassador contracts or ongoing influencer relationships.

### Podcast/PR
Dr. Jarrod Meerkin appeared on "Baseline Episode" podcast (Project 100 with Mark Bouris). Media coverage in The Senior newspaper (February 2024) about Bone Bus rural health services.

## Business Model Assessment

### Revenue model structure
MeasureUp operates a **multi-channel service business** with three distinct revenue streams:

1. **Direct-to-consumer clinics**: Higher-margin individual appointments ($109-$2,999) in Sydney and Brisbane
2. **Mobile B2B2C**: Gym and workplace visits with per-scan fees, likely at partner-negotiated rates
3. **Healthcare system (The Bone Bus)**: Medicare bulk-billing for bone density scans — lower margin but high volume; estimated 2,500+ medical centre partners create recurring distribution

The **$5.4M estimated revenue** with 20-50 employees suggests a profitable, capital-efficient operation without venture funding.

### Strengths
- **First-mover positioning**: 18+ years as Australia's DEXA pioneer creates brand authority
- **Mobile infrastructure moat**: 11-14 mobile labs with capital-intensive DEXA equipment is hard to replicate
- **Healthcare distribution**: 2,500+ medical centre partnerships via The Bone Bus create embedded referral flow
- **Research credibility**: PhD founder, peer-reviewed publications, AIS association
- **Longevity trend alignment**: Peter Attia positioning resonates with optimization-focused consumers
- **Insurance claimability**: RMR and some DEXA consults claimable through private health funds

### Weaknesses
- **No blood biomarkers**: Misses the comprehensive metabolic health market
- **No subscription model**: Each customer must re-purchase; no recurring revenue lock-in
- **No supplements/products**: Revenue limited to services only; no product margins
- **Weak digital experience**: iOS app has crashing issues; no Android app; web portal basic
- **Limited social presence**: 6K Instagram followers and 199 LinkedIn followers after 15+ years indicates weak direct-to-consumer brand building
- **Geographic constraint**: Physical scans require Australia presence; cannot expand digitally

### Notable tactics
- **The Bone Bus branding**: Separate brand identity for healthcare channel avoids diluting premium positioning
- **Medicare arbitrage**: Bulk-billing bone density scans for elderly creates volume base
- **Partner referral codes**: Simple system with 10%+ discounts incentivizes fitness professionals to refer
- **Transformation package upsell**: $2,999 offering captures highest-intent customers with comprehensive service

### Gaps and limitations for longevity market
1. No blood biomarkers (glucose, lipids, hormones, inflammation markers)
2. No at-home testing option
3. No supplement recommendations or personalization
4. No ongoing subscription for longitudinal tracking
5. No telehealth/virtual coaching
6. No AI-driven recommendations
7. Australia-only footprint

## Strategic Observations

### Positioning for Thailand longevity startup comparison

MeasureUp occupies the **body composition measurement niche** but has entirely ceded the **blood biomarker and supplement** space. A Thailand-based longevity startup could view MeasureUp as:

1. **Not a direct competitor**: Different core offering (physical composition vs. blood biomarkers)
2. **Potential partnership model**: DEXA body composition could complement blood testing for comprehensive health assessment
3. **Validation of longevity positioning**: MeasureUp's Peter Attia alignment confirms market appetite for longevity-focused wellness services
4. **What to avoid**: Weak digital experience, lack of subscription model, no supplement integration

### Key strategic takeaways

**The partnership model works in Australia**: MeasureUp's 2,500+ medical centre relationships demonstrate that B2B2C through healthcare providers can scale. A Thailand startup could similarly partner with clinics, corporate wellness programs, and fitness facilities rather than building purely D2C.

**Longevity positioning resonates**: MeasureUp's explicit alignment with Peter Attia's "Outlive" messaging indicates this positioning attracts premium customers. The $585 Longevity Package and $2,999 Transformation Package show willingness to pay for comprehensive assessment.

**Physical infrastructure creates barriers**: MeasureUp's 11-14 mobile DEXA labs represent millions in equipment investment that competitors cannot easily replicate. A blood testing startup has lower capital requirements but faces different moats (lab partnerships, regulatory approvals).

**Subscription gap is opportunity**: MeasureUp's one-time transaction model leaves recurring revenue on the table. A competitor offering subscription-based longitudinal tracking with blood biomarkers could create stronger customer retention.

**Digital experience matters more for blood testing**: MeasureUp's weak app and limited social presence hasn't prevented growth because their service requires physical presence anyway. A blood testing startup with at-home collection needs excellent digital experience to succeed.
