## Rationale for Inclusions / Exclusions

### Checkup+ (9,550 THB)
Skips everything a standard Thai hospital checkup already covers: CBC, liver panel, kidney markers, electrolytes, basic lipids, glucose, and urine. What remains is the layer hospitals never run: full hormone panel, advanced cardiovascular risk (ApoB, Lp(a)), inflammation markers, thyroid with antibodies, key micronutrients, and minerals. IGFBP-3 dropped to stay under budget — IGF-1 alone is sufficient for most action plans. The antioxidant panel (S072) and toxic metals (S261) are excluded because at 4,100 and 1,800 respectively they consume too much budget for markers that rarely drive immediate action.

### Supplement Optimization (7,250 THB)
Built around one question: what should this person take, at what dose, and is what they're already taking working? Covers the full deficiency picture (D, B12, Folate, Ferritin, Magnesium, Se/Cu/Zn), metabolic baseline (glucose, HbA1c, insulin), thyroid (affects absorption and energy), hormones (testosterone, cortisol, DHEAS), and inflammation/methylation (hs-CRP, homocysteine). CBC included to check for iron-deficiency anaemia alongside ferritin. Skips advanced cardiovascular markers (ApoB, Lp(a)) — not relevant to supplement decisions. Skips IGFBP-3 and Anti-TPO for cost.

### Longevity (8,290 THB)
The Peter Attia panel. Prioritizes cardiovascular risk (ApoB, Lp(a), full lipids), metabolic health (insulin resistance triad: glucose + HbA1c + insulin), inflammation (hs-CRP + homocysteine), hormonal aging (testosterone, DHEAS, IGF-1, cortisol), and thyroid. GGT included as a liver/oxidative stress longevity marker. Creatinine included as baseline kidney function. Skips minerals and folate to stay under 10k. Skips Anti-TPO (more diagnostic than longevity-predictive). Skips IGFBP-3 and the expensive antioxidant and toxic metals panels.
