Hey there,

A 27-year-old woman training for a marathon and a 45-year-old man with a family history of heart disease have different health needs.

Their goals are different. Their risks are different. The questions worth asking are different.

So what should *you* pay attention to?

- If heart disease runs in your family, have you tested Lp(a)?
- If brain fog or skin issues won't resolve, have you looked at your gut?
- If you live in an older home, do you know your lead levels?
- If you're over 50, are you screening for cancer beyond routine tests?

Not every test is right for everyone — and data you don't need is just noise. What matters is testing that's matched to your body, your history, and your goals.

So what should you test? Take this short quiz to build a personalized plan that actually matters for you.

## Competitive Intelligence

- **Core Offer:** Personalized testing plan via quiz-based funnel
- **Products Promoted:** Environmental Toxins, Gut Microbiome, Multi-Cancer screening
- **Messaging Strategy:** Personalization-first — not all tests for all people; unnecessary data = noise
- **Persuasion Tactics:** Scenario-based empathy (two personas), self-identification bullet questions, dual CTAs
- **Tone:** Conversational, approachable, health-literate but accessible
- **Target Signals:** Health-conscious consumers, family history concerns, symptom-driven (brain fog, skin), age-based screening (50+), environmental exposure
